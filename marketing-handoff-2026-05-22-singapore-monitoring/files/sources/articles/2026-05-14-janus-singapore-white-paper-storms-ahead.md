# Janus Digital Singapore — White paper: How agentic AI can answer the storms ahead

Janus Digital Singapore Pte. Ltd. position paper (20 pages, ~3.8 MB PDF) written in response to Prime Minister Lawrence Wong's May Day Rally 2026 speech. Co-authored by [[bonaventure-wong|Bonaventure Wong]] (Co-Founder & Global CEO) and [[joyce-woo|Joyce Woo]] (CEO, Singapore). Dated May 2026; PDF metadata creation timestamp 2026-05-14 06:15 UTC.

## Strategic positioning of this document

The paper is structured as Janus Digital's commitment to Singapore in response to the PM's "two storms" framing — (1) energy supply shock from the Strait of Hormuz closure, (2) AI-driven economic transformation. It frames Janus's platform as the answer to both, converging on the commercial built environment (33%+ of SG electricity consumption, 20%+ of national carbon emissions).

This is **the white paper Bonaventure referenced on the 12 May AI Native CEO call** — written by Janus on the back of PM Wong's May Day Rally speech (not a separate forthcoming PMO document; confirmed by Michael 2026-05-15). The upstream substrate is the PM's speech itself (1 May 2026).

## Section structure

1. **Executive summary** (p3) — convergence of four pressures on commercial real estate: 33%+ SG electricity, 20%+ emissions, ISSB-aligned disclosure FY2025, MEI regime live since Sept 2025, carbon tax 9× increase since 2023 (S$5 → S$45/tonne), Tripartite Jobs Council (announced 30 April 2026). "Four pressures, one convergence point, one twelve-month window for action."
2. **Foreword** (p4) — co-signed by Bonaventure + Joyce. "Singapore is uniquely positioned to convert both storms into a structural advantage."
3. **The first storm: Energy at the building edge** (p5–6) — platform's verified outcomes (29% energy savings, 29% productivity uplift, 29% asset lifespan extension, 50% risk reduction, 40% tenant satisfaction). Case study: 220,000 sqm flagship mall, owned by major mixed-use operator (65M sqm under management across 200+ cities, 200 retail units, 20M visits/year). Outcomes: 10% additional energy savings above baseline, 70% admin reduction, 99% fault detection, response time from 15 min → <60 s, 98% environmental compliance.
4. **The second storm: From enhanced search to a new lever** (p7) — direct quote from PM Wong about AI as "enhanced search" being "scratching the surface." Janus as physical AI for the built environment.
5. **Under the hood: The Janus operating layer** (p8–10) — the technical anchor section:
   - **5.1 Physics over pattern matching** — tokenises thermodynamics, fluid flow, and temperature dynamics (not text/pixels). ~91% realism to actual physical behaviour. "PhD in property and physical-world assets." Cold-start advantage: years of data → weeks of contextual mapping.
   - **5.2 The live BIM** — hardware-agnostic, vendor-agnostic. Native protocol translation across BACnet, Modbus, LonWorks; vendor systems including Siemens, Honeywell, Schneider, Johnson Controls, Bosch (BMS); Daikin, Trane, Carrier, York (HVAC); Lutron, Acuity, Helvar (lighting); Genetec, Honeywell, Bosch (access); Otis, KONE, Schindler, Mitsubishi (lift); Schneider, ABB, Siemens (metering). Builds BIM from 2D blueprints when none exists. Case study: ~400-room luxury beach resort — reconciled 3,000+ pieces of equipment, ~20% of BMS dormant on arrival.
   - **5.3 Cross domain micro-adjustment 24/7/365** — ingests HVAC telemetry + camera feeds + occupancy + outside humidity/temperature + weather forecasts + live energy market prices. Optimisation target varies by asset class (Grade A office = energy cost minimisation; hospitality = guest comfort envelope; healthcare = air quality / pressure differential compliance). Acts via BACnet IP where authorised; recommends-then-confirms otherwise.
   - **5.4 Onboarding methodology** — operator-led, train-the-trainer, ~2 weeks. Four phases: Audit → BIM build → Connect & benchmark → Train the trainer.
   - **5.5 Real-time sustainability disclosure** — same telemetry as energy optimisation produces continuous audit-grade emissions data. "Compliance becomes a by-product of operations, not a separate workstream."
6. **The promise: Workers as architects, not bystanders** (p11–12) — quotes PM Wong's "we may not be able to protect every job. But we will protect every worker" line. References DBS example (customer service officer 26 years ago → leads 20-person AI team). Janus model: certify existing engineers from asset operators / property managers / global hospitality groups rather than fielding a competing engineering force. Tier-1 SG real estate operator quote: *"We don't need your engineers. We have engineers. We just need to know how to connect to you."* Working through SkillsFuture + Tripartite Jobs Council framework for a national training pathway.
7. **The Singapore way, in practice: Three commitments** (p13) — to Government (engage with National AI Council, BCA 80-80-80, MEI regime, Singapore-Asia Taxonomy; verified savings auditable monthly); to the Labour Movement (AI tooling + curriculum at no cost to NTUC training arms + Tripartite Jobs Council); to Employers ("Queen Bee" framing — REITs, SWFs, pension funds, asset managers, real estate developers, global hospitality; SME ecosystem onboarded with the institution).
8. **In closing: A commitment to Singapore** (p14) — Joyce Woo named as the leader of Janus's Singapore activation. "We are hiring in Singapore. We are training in Singapore. We are paying tax in Singapore." Singapore as the operating template for every future Janus market entry.
9. **About the authors** (p15) — bios for Bonaventure + Joyce (captured below).
10. **About Janus Digital** (p16) — corporate description. 15 years R&D, 76 granted patents, 4th-generation platform, 10,000+ buildings, 253 cities, 800M+ data streams. Outcome-based commercial model.
11. **Contact** (p16) — engage@janusd.sg / janusd.sg.
12. **Disclaimer** (p17) — standard informational-purposes language. Fair-use attribution for PM quotes.
13. **References** (p17–20) — extensive bibliography (~35 sources): ACRA, BCA, Channel News Asia, CNA, MOM, NCCS, PMO Singapore, Straits Times, Business Times, Savills, Siemens, Cushman & Wakefield, plus Yann LeCun / physical AI references (Onsen Dev, Nexar appoints LeCun to board, Neurotechnus on world models), Strait of Hormuz coverage (AP, Bloomberg/Energy Connects, Washington Post, Wikipedia).
