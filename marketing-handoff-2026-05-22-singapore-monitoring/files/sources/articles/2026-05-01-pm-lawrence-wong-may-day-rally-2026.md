# PM Lawrence Wong at May Day Rally 2026

Transcript of the speech delivered by Singapore's Prime Minister and Minister for Finance Lawrence Wong at the May Day Rally 2026, held at Downtown East, D'Marquee on 1 May 2026. Audience: NTUC president & secretary-general (Brother Ng Chee Meng), SNEF president, the Labour Movement.

Fetched from `pmo.gov.sg` on 2026-05-15. Official PMO transcript; canonical text.

## Why this is in the wiki

The PM's speech is **the upstream substrate** for the [[2026-05-14-janus-singapore-white-paper-storms-ahead|Janus Singapore white paper]] — which is built on the back of this speech and quotes it extensively. The speech itself sets the policy and rhetorical anchors Janus's SG positioning runs through:

- **The "two storms" framing** — Strait of Hormuz energy crisis + AI transformation.
- **The "enhanced search vs. unlocking more" framing of AI** — "many tell me they are using it like an enhanced search tool... that is only scratching the surface."
- **"We may not be able to protect every job. But we will protect every worker. Because in Singapore, every worker matters!"** — quoted directly in the Janus paper.
- **The DBS worker-elevation example** (Ali Jinah Mohamed Yusuf, 26-year veteran now leading 20-person AI team) — referenced in Janus's "Workers as architects, not bystanders" section.
- **The Queen Bee + CTC model** (SMRT example) — referenced in Janus's "Three commitments" section.
- **Tripartism as Singapore's "special sauce"** — Janus's three-commitments structure (Government / Labour / Employers) mirrors this.

## Speech sections (PM-authored structure)

1. **Opening / acknowledgements** — thanks for last year's resilience; "we worked together, we adapted, we came through stronger."
2. **New Storm** — Middle East / Strait of Hormuz; stagflation parallels with 1970s oil shocks; IEA warning "this crisis could be even more severe than what the world experienced in the 70s." Singapore advantages: Jurong Island, Jurong Rock Caverns, refining/petrochemical industries built deliberately over decades. OPEC-cluster workers shout-out. Support package + readiness to do more.
3. **AI Transformation** — Google example: AI wrote 25% of new code 2024, 50% 2025, **75% today** (2026). Software engineers now "architects, orchestrators, system builders." AI agents era. National AI Council. Google DeepMind in Singapore (first SE Asia AI research lab, led by Yi Tay, an NTU grad now advancing Gemini). Advanced Machine Intelligence (Yann LeCun) — Singapore as one of four global offices, base in Asia for physical AI. DBS case study (Ali Jinah Mohamed Yusuf, started 1999/2000 as Customer Service Officer, today leads a 20-person AI team in DBS's Customer Centre).
4. **Support for Workers** — "We may not be able to protect every job. But we will protect every worker." **SWDA** announced (Skills and Workforce Development Agency, merging WSG + SSG, jointly overseen by MOM + MOE). Enhanced SkillsFuture + SkillsFuture Jobseeker Support Scheme. **SkillsFuture portal redesign + 6 months of free premium AI tools for course enrolees.** NTUC adding support on top.
5. **Tripartism in Action** — Company Training Committees (CTCs). Tan Tock Seng Hospital case (HSEU + nursing team built AI-powered rostering tool with Nurse Manager Mo Minyi — rostering down from 1+ hour to 15 mins). SMRT Bishan Depot case (National Transport Workers' Union, "kaizen" approach, $1,600 special Kaizen Bonus for every employee). SMRT named as a "Queen Bee" company. **Tripartite Jobs Council** established to scale CTC approach for the AI transition; Sec-Gen Ng Chee Meng to share NTUC plans in Parliament next week.
6. **Our Singapore Way** — Labour Movement's 65-year history through industrialisation, computerisation, globalisation, AFC, SARS, GFC, Covid. Tripartism's "special sauce" = trust. "Not Welfare; Workfare. Not Minimum Wage; Progressive Wages. Not unemployment insurance; Job Seeker Support."
7. **Conclusion** — Nisar Keshvani's RSAF-evacuation story from Riyadh; "Welcome home" as a promise; "Majulah NTUC, Majulah PAP, Majulah Singapura!"

## Key quotables (verbatim)

> "AI is the defining technology of our time."

> "Two years ago, AI wrote 25% of the new code for [Google]. Last year — 50%. Today, it is 75%. ... software engineers are not no longer needed. In fact, they are still in demand in Google itself — they are paying top dollar for good software engineers. But the software engineers are changing their roles. They are no longer just coding, they are now architects, orchestrators, and system builders."

> "Many tell me they are using it [AI] like an enhanced search tool. You ask a question, you get an answer. That is a start. But it is really only scratching the surface. There is so much more that can be unlocked."

> "I cannot promise that there will be no disruption. Jobs will change. Some will disappear. And the pace of change will be faster than anything we have seen before. But this I can promise you: as our economy transforms, we will create new and better jobs. **We may not be able to protect every job. But we will protect every worker. Because in Singapore, every worker matters!**"

> "Our tripartite system works. Others try to copy, they have the institutions, they have the name, but they cannot always replicate what we have. Because the special sauce, the special ingredient in Singapore is that we have built trust in one another."

> "In Singapore, it is not Welfare; it is Workfare. It is not a Minimum Wage; but Progressive Wages. And it is not unemployment insurance; but it is Job Seeker Support. And this is the Singapore Way."

> "'Welcome home'. Those two words carried a weight I had never felt before. ... They were not just a greeting. They were a promise. That wherever Singaporeans may be in the world, our nation will look after its own." — quoting Nisar Keshvani's account of his RSAF evacuation from Riyadh.

## Notable Singapore-specific anchor points

- **National AI Council** — coordinating SG's national AI effort. Goal: deep AI capabilities, drive adoption, hub for AI innovation; benefit every worker.
- **Skills and Workforce Development Agency (SWDA)** — new entity merging WSG + SSG, jointly overseen by MOM + MOE. Announced in this speech.
- **Tripartite Jobs Council** — established to scale the CTC approach for the AI transition. Already referenced in the Janus paper as "announced on 30 April 2026" — i.e. one day before this speech; suggests the formal announcement preceded the rally by a day.
- **Company Training Committees (CTCs)** — NTUC-originated, the operational mechanism for tripartite skill-building. Now the template for scaling AI transformation sector-by-sector.
- **Queen Bee companies** — Singapore policy framing the PM uses (SMRT is the named example) — large institutions that uplift their SME contractor network through shared technology adoption. Janus's "Three commitments → To Employers" section adopts this language verbatim.
- **Google DeepMind Singapore** — first SE Asia AI research lab; led by Yi Tay (NTU grad), works on Gemini.
- **Advanced Machine Intelligence** — Yann LeCun's company; Singapore is one of four global offices, base in Asia for "physical AI" — AI that understands and interacts with the real world. *This is directly resonant with Janus's positioning as physical AI for the built environment.*
- **DBS** — bank, "investing in AI more than a decade ago," now training every employee to use AI tools + build their own AI solutions. The Ali Jinah Mohamed Yusuf example: joined DBS 26 years ago after Singapore Poly → Customer Service Officer → today leads 20-person team in DBS's Customer Centre.
