The New AI Stack Is Not Really a Software Stack AI is unlikely to have a standard software stack in the traditional sense. More importantly, I don’t think “software stack” is the right mental model. AI requires a knowledge-centric architecture. Some of that knowledge is compressed into models. Some of it is supplied at the moment of use. The AI “stack,” if we want to call it that, is really: Context Model Compute Large language models compress and generalize an enormous amount of knowledge from text, images, video, software, and other forms of data. They are everywhere now: personal assistants, legal workflows, software development, and much more. Moving forward, LLMs must be trained with more diverse knowledge and made much more accessible and adaptable. This is a key motivation behind Project Tapestry from [The AI Alliance](https://www.linkedin.com/company/the-ai-alliance?trk=public_post-text). New model architectures are emerging too. World models, especially those based on JEPA, are better suited for efficient learning and application in the physical world: robotics, industrial systems, science, and beyond. Models are a generalized intelligence engine. They can reason, generate, classify, plan, and synthesize. But they are not the full source of knowledge for an application. That comes from context. Context is the scenario-specific knowledge an AI system uses at the moment of action. It is what makes an AI system actually work for a particular use case. Today, context is built many ways: prompts, [skill.md](https://www.linkedin.com/redir/redirect?url=http%3A%2F%2Fskill%2Emd&urlhash=f1-B&trk=public_post-text) files, RAG, internet search, knowledge graphs, MCP tools, humans-in-the-loop, agents calling other agents, and more. I think there is a major opportunity to unify this space into a common set of principles and architectural practices. Is there an operating system for context? A kernel for knowledge? A Kubernetes-and-containers ecosystem for knowledge? I think the Semiont project from [The AI Alliance](https://www.linkedin.com/company/the-ai-alliance?trk=public_post-text) could be a key part of this. Finally, compute is how all of this runs. It is essential fuel for AI, like energy is for transportation, cities, homes, and factories. It needs continued innovation so that AI can scale efficiently and become broadly accessible. Yes, compute needs a serious software stack. And no, it cannot just be vibe-coded. But compute and its associated software stacks should occupy no more than about a third of our mental model of an AI system. We should not glue ourselves too tightly to a software paradigm when thinking about how AI systems evolve. Context–Model–Compute is a straightforward mental picture of what an AI system is, where value lives, and where the next generation of AI-centric infrastructure may emerge. The future of AI infrastructure will not just be about serving models faster. It will be about organizing, governing, and applying knowledge better: Models + Context.
