## **The Technology: An MRI for Buildings** 

Think of Janus as an MRI for buildings and critical infrastructure. The platform creates millimetre-accurate digital twins of entire structures—every pipe, wire, and duct—and continuously monitors, in real time, how energy, water, and air flow through every system.

Traditional building management relies on reactive alerts, siloed systems, and manual optimisation. Janus replaces this entirely with:

• Predictive Intelligence: Anticipates maintenance needs days in advance, preventing failures before they happen

• Unified Dashboard: Integrates all building systems into one intelligent view for complete visibility

• Autonomous AI Optimisation: Physics-grounded AI making thousands of micro-adjustments 24/7

Unlike language-based AI models that can hallucinate, Janus is built on physics—thermodynamics, pressure dynamics, energy conservation, and fluid mechanics. The platform is powered by HGTFT (Heterogeneous Graph Temporal Fusion Transformer), a proprietary forecasting framework that combines Transformer layers for temporal pattern recognition with Graph Attention layers for modelling physical relationships—the result: 91% improvement in physical realism compared to standard models. Janus calculates—it doesn’t guess.

## **Position Overview** 

We are looking for a mid-to-senior full-stack engineer to build and own the production software at the core of Janus Digital’s AI platform, the intelligence layer that a globally distributed organisation relies on day to day. This is a product engineering role, not a scripting or automation role: your output is robust backend services, polished user-facing interfaces, and a platform foundation engineered to a banking-grade standard of reliability, security, and scale. You will join a small, established AI engineering team and own meaningful parts of the platform end to end, working at high leverage with frontier development tooling. 

**The Role**

The platform is accessed through multiple surfaces—Slack, web dashboards, and purpose-built interfaces—by users ranging from AI-native engineers to operations staff who have never opened a terminal. Both are first-class citizens, and the architecture must serve them equally.

You will join a small, established AI engineering team and work closely with existing engineers on architecture and delivery—a collaborative environment, not a solo assignment. You will also partner with the CTO and infrastructure function on cloud deployment and platform reliability.

The platform is architected around composable microservices—discrete, independently deployable services owned by small squads as the team grows. You should have built systems this way, not just monoliths.

We expect this engineer to operate at high leverage. Agentic development tools—Claude Code, Codex, and CLI-based AI workflows—are standard equipment here, used with professional discipline and judgment, not as a substitute for engineering ownership. You should already be using them daily, understand their limits, and know when to override them.

## **Key Responsibilities** 

### **Full-Stack Product Engineering** 

* **Build and own the product across the stack**: backend services and APIs in Python and TypeScript; frontend interfaces in React (or equivalent) for web dashboards, administrative surfaces, and AI agent interaction layers.

* **Design interfaces that serve the full user range**: from engineers working programmatically to operational staff using guided, purpose-built workflows—without privileging either end of the spectrum.

* Treat usability, performance, and reliability as engineering requirements on equal footing with feature delivery.

* Own features end to end—design, implementation, testing, deployment, and the ongoing operational health of what you ship. 

### 

### **Cloud, Infrastructure & Reliability** 

* Build cloud-native software on AWS (primary), and across the wider cloud ecosystem where relevant, that assumes it will run in a managed, containerised cluster environment.

* Apply containerisation (Docker, Kubernetes) as the standard deployment unit; reason fluently about networking, scaling, and resource management at the application layer.

* Engineer for reliability: observability (logging, metrics, tracing), graceful degradation, sensible SLOs, and the operational discipline expected of high-availability systems.

* Partner with the CTO and infrastructure team on deployment and platform reliability—you own the application layer, they own the infrastructure layer, and both functions must speak the same language. Terraform/IaC ownership sits with the infrastructure function; you should understand it and how it relates to what you ship, without being blocked waiting on support. 

### 

### **Enterprise Identity, Security & Access Control** 

* Design and implement enterprise-grade authentication and authorisation: SSO (SAML/OIDC), SCIM provisioning, RBAC, and fine-grained IAM.

* Architect tenant and data isolation—boundaries across data, compute, and configuration that hold firm as the platform scales.

* Implement audit logging, DLP controls, and data governance that satisfy enterprise and regulatory expectations (GDPR, Singapore PDPA, UAE data protection frameworks, and equivalents).

* Build self-service access management so administrators in distributed offices can manage their own users without depending on central support.

* Treat security as a design constraint, not a post-launch checkbox—zero-trust principles, least privilege, and defence in depth throughout. 

### 

### **Platform Architecture & Multi-Tenancy** 

* Design the platform for multi-tenancy from the ground up: configuration isolation, provisioning flows, usage metering, and the operational tooling needed to run it reliably at scale.

* Build the API surface to a production standard: versioning, authentication, rate limiting, documentation, and dependable reliability guarantees.

* Architect for global distribution—latency, regional data residency requirements, and the support and maintenance implications of a user base spread across many time zones and office locations. 

### 

### **AI Agent Development & Orchestration** 

* Build and deploy production AI agents using MCP and streaming APIs; design multi-model orchestration across Claude, Gemini, and other models as the landscape evolves.

* Manage system prompts, API boundaries, and context controls across model providers; ensure data governance holds at every layer of the agent stack. 

### 

### **Memory & Knowledge Systems** 

* Contribute to and evolve the memory and knowledge architecture at the core of the platform—a structured, multi-graph, file-based system underpinning institutional intelligence across the company. This is among the hardest and most consequential engineering problems on the platform.

* Build the integrations between the memory system and agent workflows so agents consistently access the right context regardless of which model or interface surface is in use.

* Stay current on the rapidly evolving field of AI memory and context management; evaluate emerging approaches and adapt the architecture as better patterns emerge—the system is designed to evolve, not to ossify around today’s assumptions. 

### 

### **Microservices Architecture** 

* Architect the platform as a composition of isolated, independently deployable microservices—each functional domain (finance tools, marketing stack, knowledge layer, agent runtime) a discrete service with well-defined APIs between them.

* Design for composability: services should be replaceable and extensible without requiring platform-wide rebuilds; as the team grows, individual squads should own discrete services without stepping on each other.

**Success Metrics**   
Performance in this role is measured against outcomes, not activity. In practice, success means:

* **Delivery & ownership —** ships production features end to end on a predictable cadence, and owns a core service or domain independently within the first 90 days.

* **Reliability —** services owned meet agreed availability and SLO targets, with few user-facing incidents traceable to owned code and a fast mean-time-to-recovery when they do occur.

* **Quality —** low defect-escape rate, meaningful automated test coverage on owned services, and code that consistently passes review without repeated rework.

* **Security & compliance —** no unresolved critical or high-severity findings in owned code; identity, access, audit-logging, and data-governance controls hold up to internal and regulatory review.

* **Architecture —** design decisions remain sound as scope and load grow; services stay independently deployable and composable, reducing rather than adding long-term complexity.

* **AI capability —** AI agents and integrations reach production with reliable multi-model orchestration and sound data governance, including a measurable contribution to the memory/knowledge and agent-integration layers.

* **Leverage & collaboration —** consistently high output through disciplined use of agentic tooling, and effective partnership with the AI Office, CTO, and infrastructure functions—unblocking both self and others.
