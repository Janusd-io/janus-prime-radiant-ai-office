# AI public backlash strengthens in the US (May 2026)

Two mid-May 2026 pieces — WSJ "The American Rebellion Against AI Is Gaining Steam" and NYT "The Generation That Grew Up With A.I. Hates It" — document a hardening of US public sentiment against AI, particularly among young people.

## Key signals

- **Polling:** Only 18% of Gen Z feels hopeful about AI; nearly half say risks outweigh benefits (Gallup). 47% of US voters under 30 rate AI as "mostly bad" — highest of any age bracket (NYT/Siena).
- **Public reactions:** Eric Schmidt booed at University of Arizona commencement when mentioning AI. Similar boos at University of Central Florida a week earlier. Pop-culture villainisation (HBO's "Hacks", actor Hannah Einbinder calling AI builders "losers").
- **Political mobilisation:** Bernie Sanders, AOC, James Fishback calling for data-centre moratoriums. AI super-PACs becoming dominant political players.
- **Isolated violence:** Molotov cocktail thrown at Sam Altman's home (cited in NYT piece).
- **Trust gap:** Stanford research — across 30 countries, Americans had the LEAST faith in their leaders' ability to regulate AI.

## Why it matters for the AIO

- The KPMG/Anthropic deal and the JPMorgan/StanChart/Meta layoff cycle are landing into this backdrop, not an indifferent one — see [[2026-05-25-ai-labor-restructuring-bank-and-tech-wave]]. Enterprise narratives need to anticipate the public sentiment, not assume it.
- For [[ai-native-janus-positioning]] (Marketing): the marketing posture cannot be uncritically enthusiastic. The Janus brand voice probably needs to acknowledge the legitimate criticisms while differentiating on substance. The Andrew-Soane satirical framing surfaced at [[ai-washing]] is one available angle.
- For internal AIO use: the same backlash is showing up in client conversations (anecdotal). Worth raising with [[andrew-soane]] for the Marketing Prime Radiant.

## Watch for

- Backlash spreading from US to UK / EU markets where Janus operates.
- Regulatory responses (state-level US AI moratoriums; EU AI Act enforcement).
- Counter-narrative formation — NYT piece cites Japan and Nordic governments using policy to ensure AI benefits workers, producing more pro-AI local sentiment. The "American problem" framing is an angle Janus can engage with internationally.
