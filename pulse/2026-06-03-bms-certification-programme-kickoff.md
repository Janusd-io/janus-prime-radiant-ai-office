# BMS Engineer Certification Programme — kickoff and Emma McCall intro

On 3 June 2026, Bonaventure Wong and Michael Bruck held an introductory onboarding meeting with Emma McCall, who is joining Janus as the training and certification programme lead. The meeting marks the formal start of the BMS Engineer Certification Programme — Janus's first structured external certification for on-site engineers operating the Janus AI for Buildings platform.

## What happened

Emma McCall was introduced to: the certification programme mandate (BMS engineers, Singapore-first, train-the-trainer model), the Prime Radiant knowledge base via live demo, and the team she'll be working with (Bonaventure, Michael, and a Janus delivery/PM team member who has deep platform build history).

The Prime Radiant demo was a live query: "how should we approach development of a certification program for BMS engineers." The wiki surfaced the CEO meeting decision on certification, the Lysander 28-phase workflow, and other meeting context — demonstrating the system's value to Emma directly. Bonaventure: *"I've already forgotten about that"* (referring to the CEO certification decision from prior weeks, which the wiki surfaced instantly).

## Why this matters

This is the first external-facing use of the Prime Radiant pattern — the wiki is now the knowledge substrate for a programme that will eventually onboard engineers across 10 countries. The compounding value of the institutional memory is being demonstrated outwardly, not just internally.

It also surfaces a second workstream: **internal Janus AI tool training**. Bonaventure named this as *"a big missing piece in our onboarding"* — staff are joining faster than training can keep up with, and misuse of tools (e.g. Claude for PowerPoint) is already creating cost spikes. Emma's mandate covers this alongside the BMS programme.

## Key signals

- **"Certification not training"** — deliberate framing to avoid displacement optics. The programme positions itself as bridging engineers' existing expertise to Janus's platform, not replacing anything.
- **Build-not-buy for training infrastructure** — Bonaventure and Michael explicitly discussed not adopting an LMS. The Prime Radiant + AI agents is the candidate substrate for personalised learning paths and certification delivery.
- **Singapore first** — Keppel building as PoC; union context makes the "no displacement" message essential. This is also the first market where the certification programme will be validated before UK and remaining 8 countries.
- **Internal training gap** — most Janus staff not using AI tools at appropriate capability levels. Anti-PowerPoint via Claude is the example symptom; the root cause is lack of structured onboarding on which tools for which tasks.
