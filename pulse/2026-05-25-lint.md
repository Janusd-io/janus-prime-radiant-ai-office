`vendors/` at the top level contains 99 files; `entities/vendors/` (the §2-canonical location) has 37. Spot-check of overlap: of the 12 names present in both locations (viktor, linear, claude, obsidian, etc.), all have **divergent content with different sizes** — they are NOT identical mirrors. This is a real schema-conformance issue, not just a lint artifact.

Hypotheses for how this happened (need curator confirmation):

- The top-level `vendors/` folder may have been auto-populated from the Linear AIR registry as an experimental sync (87 of the 99 files have AIR-style vendor names like `chatgpt-monitor-instance`, `attio-duplicate-air-76`, `import-your-data-linear-placeholder`).
- The 12 overlap files may be where curators independently expanded the entity entry inside `entities/vendors/` (the canonical location) while the AIR sync continued populating the top-level shadow.

**Proposed workstream** (needs Michael's sign-off before execution):

1. Audit the 99 top-level `vendors/` files: categorise as (a) AIR-import artifact (delete or migrate), (b) divergent-from-entities (merge), (c) net-new vendor not yet in entities (move into `entities/vendors/`).
2. Decide whether `vendors/` becomes a sanctioned secondary mount (e.g., for AIR live-sync that hasn't been promoted to wiki yet — different semantics from curated entity pages) or gets deleted outright.
3. Update `index.md` to reflect the canonical location.

Scope estimate: 99 files × ~3 min triage each = ~5 hours focused work. Not for inline lint resolution.

### C.2 — Marketing-handoff slug resolution (NEW)

Slugs like `ai-native-janus-positioning` are referenced by live wiki pages but only physically exist inside `marketing-handoff-2026-05-22-broader-marketing-corpus/files/briefs/`. The handoff folder is presumably immutable (it's a frozen package for Andrew), so the references can't be made to resolve by moving files.

**Two reasonable options:**

A) **Mirror selected briefs back into `briefs/`** — copies of the handoff briefs live as live wiki entries, kept in sync manually. Loses "the handoff was a snapshot" semantics but resolves references.

B) **Treat handoff slugs as a sanctioned cross-vault reference** — same convention as Linear AIR IDs (`air-103`) being non-filesystem slugs. Lint script learns to whitelist `marketing-handoff-*` slugs. Preserves handoff-as-snapshot semantics but slugs feel broken to readers who try to follow them.

Curator decision needed.

### C.3 — `inbox/.processed/` audit-trail discipline (NEW)

The §5.1 ingest rule says "Move the original from `inbox/` to `inbox/.processed/<YYYY-MM>/` for audit. Never delete." The 2026-05-25 batch-ingest used a single `mv` per file that relocated content directly from `inbox/` to `sources/articles/` with a kebab-case rename. No copy was placed in `inbox/.processed/`. Git history tracks the moves; the batch-ingest log entry lists the filed slugs. Functionally lossless.

**Worth codifying which is canonical:**
- (a) The §5.1 strict reading: keep an archive copy in `.processed/` AND the canonical-named file in `sources/`. Provides explicit audit at the cost of duplication.
- (b) The pragmatic reading observed today: one `mv` per file, audit lives in git + log.md. Lighter; relies on git not being rewritten (which CLAUDE.md §5 git-awareness already mandates).

Both have rationale. The next CLAUDE.md schema bump should pick one and commit.
