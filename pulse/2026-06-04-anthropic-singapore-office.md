# Anthropic opens a Singapore presence — finance, product support, and a regional research economist

Anthropic advertised four Singapore-based roles on 2026-06-04 (Business Times, Sarah Koh; [[anthropic-singapore-office]]), its first visible move toward a Singapore office. Follows OpenAI and Google DeepMind, both of which already run SG labs. Directly relevant to Janus given `sg` is the lead market (opens W19; see the [[2026-05-12-singapore-as-lead-market|lead-market decision]] in the Singapore monitoring handoff).

## The roles

- **APAC head of accounting** — builds/runs regional corporate accounting across multiple APAC locations; reports into the Dublin international-accounting head.
- **Two product support specialists** — become experts across Anthropic's product range and provide user support.
- **Regional research economist** — works with governments, academics, and industry to *measure AI's effects on the economy* and explore policy interventions. Requires an economics PhD with causal-inference / ML empirical methods + Python. Advertised salary S$307,200–331,200 incl. commissions. All roles require ≥25% in-office.

Anthropic declined to comment on office plans. It has 12 offices globally (Japan, South Korea, India, Australia, …) since 2021.

1. **The research-economist role is the SG-local extension of the [[observed-exposure-ai-labor-measure|observed-exposure labor work]].** Anthropic is now staffing first-party AI-labor-impact economics *in-region*, working directly with governments. For Janus's SG positioning — where the [[2026-05-12-vivian-balakrishnan-llm-wiki-government|government-facing angle]] is live — this means the AI-labor-measurement narrative Janus references will increasingly have a Singapore-resident Anthropic counterpart producing it. Worth tracking for the Marketing/Office-of-CEO positioning.
2. **Vendor-proximity upside.** A local Anthropic accounting + support presence lowers the friction of Janus's Claude-first stack in-region (support, procurement, account contact in the same timezone). Minor but real for the [[ai-native-enterprise-restructuring|enterprise-restructuring]] operating posture.
3. **GIC backing reinforces the SG thread.** Singapore's sovereign wealth fund GIC first invested Sept 2025 and led the Feb 2026 US$30B Series G; the subsequent Series H puts Anthropic at **US$965B — ahead of OpenAI's US$852B**. A Singapore-state financial stake plus a Singapore office is a coherent signal that Anthropic treats SG as a strategic node, not just a sales territory.
