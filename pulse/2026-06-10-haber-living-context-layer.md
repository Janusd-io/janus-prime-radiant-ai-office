# Haber / a16z — "The living context layer"

David Haber (a16z) published an opinion piece on 10 June 2026 naming meeting recording as an irreversible default and coining "the living context layer" — the new enterprise system of record built from voice/conversation rather than structured text. The piece is direct external validation of the Prime Radiant thesis, from an investor vantage point.

## Why this matters to Janus / AIO

The argument Haber makes is structurally identical to what the Prime Radiant system is doing: the model that has ingested two years of internal conversation is a better assistant than the one that read only documentation. He names Bridgewater and OpenAI as ahead-of-the-curve practitioners and Granola as the clearest current example — because it has been in the room.

Janus is doing exactly this. The Fireflies-based meeting ingest → Prime Radiant synthesis loop is the "living context layer" Haber describes. The Bonaventure moment from the Emma McCall meeting (*"I've already forgotten about that"* — on the CEO certification decision, which the wiki surfaced instantly) is the same compounding value Haber identifies.

The AIO is also a **verbal culture** organisation, which Haber says has the highest compounding advantage from AI: verbal culture companies had a bottleneck (conversation context evaporated) that AI now solves. Janus's all-hands-in-one-room operating style means Prime Radiant is capturing more dense signal per person than a remote-first company would.

## Key claims worth tracking

- The default will flip to "assume you're being recorded unless explicitly designated otherwise." Janus is already operating on this default.
- **AC Priv** designation likely for HR/legal carve-outs — relevant for Emma McCall's internal training programme (sensitive onboarding conversations).
- The living context layer is a new software category. Haber sees it as a large enterprise opportunity; no obvious winner yet. Granola as the clearest current product.
