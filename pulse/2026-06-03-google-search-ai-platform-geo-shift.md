# Google reimagines Search as an AI platform — and triggers the GEO/ad-market shift

The Google I/O 2026 search announcement (already filed at [[2026-05-19-google-io-2026-agents-as-product]] from the developer-platform angle) lands here from two follow-on angles: TechCrunch's mechanics of the Search overhaul (Sarah Perez, 2026-05-19, [[google-search-as-you-know-it-is-over]]) and Bloomberg's read on the advertising/publisher fallout (Parmy Olson, 2026-06-03, [[googles-ai-shift-collective-freak-out]]). The "ten blue links" era is over: Search is becoming an AI surface that *acts* rather than *lists*, and the second-order effect — a new discovery-optimisation discipline (GEO) and a freaking-out online-ad market — is the part most relevant to Janus's Marketing instance.

## What changed (the mechanics)

Per [[google-search-as-you-know-it-is-over]]:

- **The intelligent search box** — biggest change to the search entry point in 25+ years. The box widens for conversational queries; an AI query-suggestion system goes beyond autocomplete; AI Mode supports follow-ups inline. Links aren't gone, but they're no longer the priority for many query types.
- **Information agents** — user-created agents that run 24/7 to track web changes and synthesise updates (Google's example: track market movements on set parameters). An AI-native evolution of Google Alerts (2003) — beyond spotting changes, they make sense of them. Rolls out to AI Pro/Ultra subscribers this summer.
- **Generative UI** — Search builds custom interactive widgets/visualisations on the fly (Liz Reid's black-hole example), built with DeepMind on **Gemini Flash 3.5**, free to all users this summer. Stateful "project spaces" you return to.
- **Mini-apps in Search** — natural-language app building inside Search via [[google-antigravity|Antigravity]] (meal-planner pulling from your calendar, custom fitness app). Action, not retrieval.

Scale anchors: AI Overviews now >2.5B monthly users; AI Mode >1B monthly (vs ChatGPT's ~900M weekly per the article's own comparison).

## Why this matters to the AI Office / Marketing instance

1. **GEO is the new SEO — and it's a Marketing-instance concern, not just an AIO curiosity.** Per Bloomberg, the emerging discipline is *GEO* (Generative Engine Optimization): to get cited by AI answers, brands must seed *conversations* about themselves across published articles, Trustpilot, and forums (Reddit especially — a favoured scraping source). The old backlink/keyword PageRank game is being replaced by sentiment-and-mentions across the open web. WPP's Daniel Hulme: *"Humans tend to buy using emotion, but AI agents will be more rational."* This is a durable new concept worth a dedicated page — escalated for review (see [[ingest-2026-06-04-1030-generative-engine-optimization-concept]]) because of a slug collision with the existing geographic "geo" usage in [[agentic-lean-marketing-stack]].

2. **Conversational discovery ads inside AI answers.** Bloomberg: ads will sit *inside* the AI answer, potentially with a chat agent, so the user "never clicks through." Even Google insiders are noncommittal on what this means. For Janus Marketing's own go-to-market, the channel mix assumption (rank on Google → drive clicks to owned pages) is the thing under threat — worth folding into the [[agentic-lean-marketing-stack|lean marketing stack]] thinking as it matures.

3. **Publisher referral collapse accelerates.** AI Overviews already cut publisher referrals; generative UI + information agents will further "decimate" them. Ad-dependent media operations have already folded. Relevant to any Janus content/owned-media strategy that assumes search-referral traffic.

## Watch for

- **UK regulator pushback (2026-06-03).** A British regulator has ordered Google to let website owners block their content from AI search features. Publishers are split — defensive measure vs missing a new route to eyeballs. The opt-out/opt-in regime is the policy variable that will shape GEO economics; `gb` is in-scope for Janus (opens W20).
- **Whether the ad market actually shrinks.** Ad-tech attendees describe "the ground moving under them"; some expect the global internet-ad market to shrink. Counter-read: Goldman's Eric Sheridan argues Alphabet grows anyway on cloud, with ads a smaller slice. Alphabet ad revenue was still ~$77B in Q1 2026 (+16% YoY).
- **The $80B raise.** Alphabet is raising $80B in equity for AI compute (incl. $10B from Berkshire Hathaway) — the old ad-profit engine is funding the switch that partly cannibalises it. A signal of how expensive the agentic-search transition is even for the incumbent.
- **Whether smaller third-party AI tools become ad/product-placement channels.** Anthropic has said it won't put ads in Claude; OpenAI says ads won't be woven into ChatGPT answers — but smaller AI companion/concierge apps will. A fragmenting ad surface to track for Marketing.
