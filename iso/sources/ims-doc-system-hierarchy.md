# Management System Documentation Hierarchy

## L1 · Strategic Level: Policies & Objectives

**POLICIES:** Documented information established by top management that is appropriate to the purpose and context of the organization, provides a framework for setting objectives, and includes a commitment to satisfy applicable requirements and to continual improvement.

**OBJECTIVES:** Documented information defining results to be achieved. Must be consistent with the policy, measurable (if practicable), take into account applicable requirements, be monitored, communicated, and updated as appropriate.

## L2 · System and Process Level: IMS Description & Architecture

**MANAGEMENT SYSTEM MANUAL:** Documented information providing an overview of the management system, which dictates the structure, format, content, or method of presentation for documenting the management system.

**PROCESS MAPS:** Documented information necessary to support the operation of processes. Determines the inputs required and the outputs expected, and the sequence and interaction of these processes.

**ORGANIZATIONAL CHARTS:** Documented information representing the structure of the organization, showing responsibilities, authorities, and interrelationships.

## L3 · Operational Level: Procedures & Work Instructions

**PROCEDURES:** Maintained documented information specifying the established way to carry out an activity or a process.

**WORK INSTRUCTIONS:** Maintained documented information providing detailed descriptions of how to perform and record specific tasks.

## L4 · Data Capture Level: Forms & Checklists

**FORMS AND CHECKLISTS:** Documented information developed and maintained to record data demonstrating compliance with the requirements of the management system.

## L5 · Evidence Level: Records

**RETAINED DOCUMENTED INFORMATION / RECORDS:** Documented information retained for providing evidence of results achieved. Provides objective evidence that processes are being carried out as planned and that the management system is operating effectively.

## L6 · External Documentation Level

**DOCUMENTED INFORMATION OF EXTERNAL ORIGIN:** Documented information determined by the organization to be necessary for the planning and operation of the management system, which must be identified as appropriate and controlled.
