# AI's bounded role in project management — 30–60%, first-draft only

## What we learned

Lysander Liu, walking through the end-to-end PM digital delivery workflow on 14 May 2026, set a clear and defensible upper bound on where AI helps in PM work:

> "AI can help complete the pre-authorisation phase, the project basic information auto-filling. My personal judgment: between thirty percent and sixty percent. Not enough."

> "AI only produces drafts. AI generates the relevant planning documents — output drafts. After that, it's people who do the work."

And the core principle:

> "Don't completely trust what AI writes for you... I think this process — the PM's understanding of the project — is the most important phase. If you understand it correctly, you don't go off course later. If you cut corners here, all the risks happen at the end of the project, and project risks happening at the end of the project are catastrophic."

## Why this matters

Three operational consequences for how the [[janus-prime-radiant-build|Prime Radiant]] rollout treats PM (and by extension, every department where there is real project liability):

### 1. The 30–60% ceiling is a PM-set scope, not an AI capability ceiling

The number isn't about what models can do — it's about what the PM is *prepared to delegate*. Lysander explicitly excludes:

- **Organisational structure** — built only after the team is constructed; cannot be AI-imagined.
- **Construction / equipment material lists** — even if AI can describe the scope, "what to actually use, how to actually do it — AI can't give that."
- **The PM's own comprehension of the project** — this is by design not delegated.

For PM Prime Radiant initialisation, this means we don't aim for "AI does PM"; we aim for "AI gives PM the strongest first draft and the cleanest synthesis surface."

### 2. First-draft-only is the contract

Lysander's explicit framing: AI generates draft documents, *people* do the work. Spike's follow-up pushed: *"It doesn't really need to do part of the check on the draft, does it? The whole thing has to be checked over?"* Lysander: *"Yes. You cannot completely trust what AI writes for you."*

For Prime Radiant outputs that land in front of PM (charter drafts, scope documents, training manual bases, configuration drafts): assume PM will rewrite at least the substance, and frame AI output explicitly as "draft for your review" not "ready."

### 3. The understanding phase is non-negotiable

Lysander placed Phase 2 (project charter) and Phase 3 (supplementary information loop) of the [[pm-digital-delivery-workflow|PM workflow]] as the most critical: *"PM must invest, and think independently."*

This is where the AI-Native pitch (per [[ai-native-janus-positioning|Janus's three-pillar messaging]]) actually lands: Pillar 3 — augmenting and upskilling the worker — *not* replacing the worker. Lysander's bounded view of AI maps cleanly onto Pillar 3's framing: the PM remains the substantive author of the project; AI accelerates the preparation, never the comprehension.

## What this means for the Prime Radiant rollout

Threads through several places:

- **The PM instance is initialised against [[pm-digital-delivery-workflow|the workflow]]** — but the *first-draft only* discipline is built into the operating norms of the instance from day one.
- **AI's role is bounded by phase** — Phase 3 (gap identification), Phase 16 (test scenario generation), Phase 25 (training material drafts) are the sweet spots. Phase 2 (project comprehension) and Phase 5 (PM planning) are PM-only.
- **PM corrections feed the brain** — Lysander's "the brain gets sharper in direct proportion to how often we correct it" is in [[ai-native-janus-positioning|the company intro deck]] and aligns with his stance.
- **Verification standards stay PM-owned** — Phase 7 (specialised consulting) defines the verification standard. AI does not get a vote here.

## Why this is non-obvious

The default framing in mid-2026 industry discourse is "AI replaces PM" or "AI runs the project." Lysander's view is the more durable one: AI accelerates the prep work that PM was always going to delegate to junior staff anyway; PM retains the high-leverage comprehension and decision work that the project's outcome depends on. The 30–60% number reflects this honest accounting.

The trap to avoid: pitching the Prime Radiant to PM as "the AI will write your project charter for you." Pitch it as: "the AI will give you a clean first draft of the boring 30–60% so you can spend your time on the 40–70% that actually determines whether the project succeeds." That framing matches Lysander's read of his own work — and PM will recognise it as honest.
