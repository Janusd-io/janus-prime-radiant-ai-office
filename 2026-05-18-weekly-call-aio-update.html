01 · Last week — what changed and why it matters

Three implications, in plain terms.

A week of mechanical work that adds up to a different posture for the programme as a whole.

01
The foundation our internal AI work sits on is now solid enough for any department to plug in.
We moved the company's institutional-knowledge platform off Google Drive onto a more reliable, security-aware foundation. The earlier friction — the kind that slowed Andrew's onboarding — is resolved at the structural level. Every future department joins on the same clean, repeatable setup.
What this means Rollout speed across Janus is no longer constrained by the tooling. It's constrained only by which department is ready next, and by how much time we spend with them.
02
Janus has its first cross-department instance ready to launch.
Spent an hour with Lysander capturing exactly how Project Management's delivery discipline runs end-to-end. That captured workflow is what the Project Management instance gets initialised against this week — meaning the system arrives pre-loaded with their language, their decisions, their gates. It's the first real test that the pattern travels beyond the AI Office.
What this means The AI Office is no longer the only department on the system. By end of May we expect three live instances. Other departments are queued.
03
Our daily knowledge now compounds without manual handoffs.
The AI Office's daily standup now deposits its outcomes straight into the institutional record, automatically. This closes the last big manual step before the Notion sunset at the end of this month. From here, AIO's institutional memory grows whether anyone curates it or not.
What this means The system gets smarter every day with zero curator effort. The same mechanism is what every department instance inherits when it comes online.
Stakeholder signal worth noting Bonaventure flagged on the 11 May standup that the Prime Radiant rollout is "happening quicker than I thought." Validates pacing and is a leading indicator that executive backing is forming as the rollout reaches non-AIO departments.

03 · Open discussion

Four decisions I'd like us to land on the call.

Each of these is small in isolation but unlocks a meaningful next step.

Topic Context What I'd like
What do we call this system, in plain language? Inside the AI Office we call it Janus Prime Radiant. For non-technical audiences across the firm, that name needs softening — Bonaventure's read was "very geeky." The system name stays; the user-facing label is open. Andrew should be in this conversation too. A twenty-minute decision on the call.
Are we on the right pace, or should we accelerate? Three departments live by end of May if Project Management lands cleanly. HR, Finance, ISO, and the Office of the CEO are next in the queue. Bonaventure's 11 May read was that the work is happening faster than he expected — we want to calibrate whether to compress the queue further or hold pace. A read on cadence from the CEO.
How forcefully do we land the Notion sunset? End-of-May target is locked from the AI Office side. Departments still using Notion as a system of record after that date need to move. This is a coordination ask — the AI Office can run the migration, but the move-or-lose signal needs to come from leadership. Confirmation of the cutover, and who relays it to department heads.
When does the "personal vault" feature come back? The original vision included a personal vault for every employee that feeds into their department's vault. We've parked that for now — it works for one vault, breaks down at two — and team-level vaults are progressing in the meantime. We want a timing call: revisit next month, next quarter, or treat as a longer-term roadmap item? A timing call.
