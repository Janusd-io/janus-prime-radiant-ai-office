# BMS Engineer Certification Programme

Janus's formal programme to certify BMS (Building Management System) engineers — on-site/delivery personnel who install, configure, and maintain the Janus AI for Buildings platform at customer sites — in AI tool proficiency and Janus-platform fluency.

Kicked off 3 June 2026 with the onboarding of Emma McCall as training and certification programme lead (person page pending: [[ingest-2026-06-10-emma-mccall-person-page]]). Bonaventure, Michael, and a Janus delivery team member (unattributed) were present for the kickoff meeting — see [[2026-06-03-emma-mccall-intro]].

## Why this programme exists

Janus's go-to-market model depends on BMS engineers in every market being able to operate the Janus AI platform. These engineers have deep domain expertise in building systems; the gap is AI tool literacy and Janus-platform fluency. The certification bridges that gap at scale — across 10 countries by end of 2026.

Positioning framing (per Bonaventure, 3 Jun): this is a *certification*, not *training* — deliberately. Framing as certification avoids displacement optics. Engineers are not being displaced or retrained; they are being certified to connect their existing expertise to the Janus platform.

## Target audience

**Primary (Phase 1):** BMS engineers — on-site delivery tier. Domain expertise in BMS confirmed; they know the physical systems. The programme certifies their AI tool and Janus-platform layer.

**Secondary (concurrent):** Internal Janus staff — AI tool effectiveness. Identified as a gap in the 3 Jun meeting; most staff not using tools efficiently. Emma's mandate includes this, though BMS engineers are the primary deliverable.

## Geographic rollout

1. **Singapore** — first market. Keppel building as PoC/guinea pig. Singapore's tight union context means the "no displacement" messaging is load-bearing. Bonaventure: *"we are very adamant because of union that we will not leave any workers behind."*
2. **UK** — next. Bonaventure visiting post-Singapore.
3. **Remaining markets** — phased through end of 2026 (10 countries).

## Programme design (as of 3 Jun 2026)

**Curriculum architecture:** Emma McCall's domain. The knowledge base (Prime Radiant / [[janus-prime-radiant-build]]) feeds the programme — agents surface institutional knowledge so Emma can build curriculum without reading every document manually.

**Delivery model:** "Train the trainer" — senior engineers first, who then train junior/apprentice engineers. Bonaventure: *"we saturate senior engineers who will retire with a nice paycheck; importantly, how do we drive the younger apprenticeship through this program."*

**Assessment model:** Continuous assessment throughout + benchmark gap-closing test at the end. Gamification elements proposed: LinkedIn certification badges, level progression. Bonaventure: *"instead of being stuck, they'll want to get to the next level."*

**Training environment:** A real building used as a simulation/sandbox — engineers practice platform operations without affecting live systems. Already partially built per the delivery team.

**Format direction:** classroom + e-learning hybrid; labs/hands-on component prioritised (Michael's preference from his Microsoft AI course experience — *"the most important and useful part is actually the lab"*). Video generation via AI is on the table.

## Knowledge base integration

The Prime Radiant is the subject matter expert substrate. From [[2026-06-03-emma-mccall-intro]]:
- Michael demo'd a live query: "how should we approach the development of a certification program for BMS engineers" — the wiki surfaced the CEO meeting decision on certification, the Lysander 28-phase delivery workflow, and meeting context from multiple sessions.
- Bonaventure: *"don't be daunted that you have to read everything — it's already read by the AI."*

The build-not-buy posture extends to the training platform itself. Bonaventure and Michael explicitly discussed not adopting an LMS. The Prime Radiant + AI agents is the candidate substrate for both knowledge delivery and personalised learning paths.

## Internal AI training workstream

Concurrent with the BMS programme, Emma was engaged to address internal Janus staff AI tool training:
- Current gap: staff using Claude for PowerPoint (expensive anti-pattern) and not using tools at the right level. Bonaventure: *"a big missing piece in our onboarding."*
- Bonaventure identified this gap explicitly: onboarding is happening faster than training can keep up with.
- Resolution for HTML decks already in place (Janus HTML Deck skill); Emma needs to formalise how staff are trained on which tools for which tasks.
