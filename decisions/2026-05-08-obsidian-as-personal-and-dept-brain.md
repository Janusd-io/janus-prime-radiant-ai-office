# Adopt Obsidian as the substrate for personal and department-level Janus brains with shared-folder federation

## Decision

Janus will use Obsidian as the local-first personal and department knowledge vault, with shared folders mediating cross-department access (e.g. AI Ops + IT shared folder for Euclid-attended meetings) and all instances rolling up to a higher-level system that sees the entire organisation.

## Why

Obsidian runs locally so no cloud dependency is required, the vault model maps naturally to per-person, per-department, and shared scopes, and the shared-folder mechanism gives a clean way to handle cross-department visibility without rebuilding access control. Pinecone was raised as an industrial alternative for scale but Obsidian is the current call.

## Evidence

> Speaker 3: Every individual gets Obsidian. It's nice because it's an app that runs on your computer. You don't even need to run it in the cloud.
