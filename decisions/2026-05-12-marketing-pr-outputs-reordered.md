## Decision

Marketing Prime Radiant Outputs are reordered in the onboarding plan and across all derivative scope artefacts:

1. **Plans & campaigns** (was 3rd, now 1st)
2. **Briefs & positioning** (was 2nd, now 2nd)
3. **POVs, thought leadership, white papers & assets** (was 1st, now 3rd)

The deck Michael generated for Andrew's onboarding had POVs / thought leadership / white papers as the first-listed Output category. Andrew explicitly requested the reordering to anchor the Marketing PR's scope as a *full marketing function*, not as a white-paper-generation engine.

## Andrew on the call (12 May 2026)

> "Every time I've heard Bonaventure talk about this, he thinks it's just going to — all we're doing this for is to create white papers like the one that we're doing manually at the moment or semi manually... Marketing is not just about — I'm not. If I thought the job I was hired to do was just be a churn — white papers, I'd kill myself. I want to kill myself."

And on the operational reason for the reorder:

> "That order is going to be important just for the positioning. Internally."

## Why this matters

The Marketing PR is being stood up against [[bonaventure-wong|Bonaventure]]'s pull. Bonaventure's mental model of marketing-output gravitates to white papers — that's what he can immediately picture as "the thing." Andrew's framing puts campaign and plan production first, with white papers as one of several content surfaces feeding into them. The deck's category ordering is read-by-stakeholders as priority signalling; the reorder is a working-relationship-management move as much as a scope clarification.

This reordering is the *Outputs-layer* expression of the [[ai-native-janus-positioning|three-pillar messaging spine]]: campaigns and plans are the carriers of the Society / Business / Individual pillars across audiences; positioning and briefs are the spine that holds them coherent; white papers / POVs are one delivery format the campaigns can use, not the destination.

## Options considered

1. **Keep the deck's original POV-first ordering** — leaves Bonaventure-friendly framing on top but mis-represents Andrew's scope of work.
2. **Reorder as above** (chosen) — accurately represents the scope while still showing Bonaventure-resonant outputs (white papers) inside the third pillar.
3. **Remove white papers entirely** — too aggressive; white papers remain part of the Output mix, just not the headline.

## When this took effect

12 May 2026 — agreed verbally on the call. Operationalised:

- [[2026-05-11-andrew-onboarding-plan]] brief (Andrew's onboarding plan, in the AIO × Marketing mesh subfolder) was originally ordered POV-first; updated post-meeting to match this decision.
- [[marketing-prime-radiant]] project hub Architecture / Outputs-layer section reflects the new ordering.
- When Andrew presents the deck to Bonaventure or the broader team, the new ordering is the canonical version.
