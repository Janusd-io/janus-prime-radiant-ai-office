# Single gems.com domain with country sub-paths, not standalone country sites

## Decision

Country marketing will live under one gems.com (with vanity URLs like gems.sg redirecting to gems.com/singapore), rather than building separate standalone websites per country.

## Why

Standalone per-country sites would be a 'fool's economy' — every global brand serves country/language variants from one CMS with a country/language switcher. Vanity URLs preserve local positioning (Singapore IP, gems.sg in email signatures) without paying the build/maintenance tax of N separate sites. Andrew flagged he expects to have to defend this against Bonaventure.

## Evidence

> Andrew Soane: I just think that's a fool's economy. I think we have a standard Gems.com... the .sg should point to a Singapore set of pages.
