# Finance / contracts are out of scope for the tool-evaluation process

## Decision

Pricing, contracts, and legal review are explicitly excluded from the AI Office tool-evaluation process and handed off to finance/legal once a tool is approved.

## Why

Tool evaluation is about technical fit and knowledge of the tool, not commercial terms. Cost should not be a hard gate (expense may be justified), and contracts/legal are a different department's output. Keeping them out keeps the evaluation flow focused.

## Evidence

> Speaker 1: I don't think we should have a hard gate because it's about knowledge. ... The contracts and all that is legal.
