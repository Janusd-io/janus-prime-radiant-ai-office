## Decision

Singapore is Janus's primary lead commercial market for AI-Native products and the "Uber for engineers" line. UAE is deprioritised for streaming-economy products specifically (banking infrastructure constraint), though Janus's commercial presence in UAE continues for other purposes.

## Bonaventure on the call (12 May 2026)

> "I had hopes of UAE but, you know, the banking system to move from a batch economy to streaming economy is still not there. It's a big overhaul. Fortunately the banking is not an open banking due to a few families are controlling it. So maybe Singapore is the place that we double down on, you know, introducing new kind of products or features like the Uber for engineers. This is possible there."

> "Part of my trip to Singapore is including, of course, closing our investment but it's to talk to these asset managers at this place of where they're looking at — which the government, the white paper will share with you guys literally from the PM has spoken out saying that how, you know, AI is not a chatgpt but more delivering the next iteration of really now forcing REITs to use it not just on the side but integrated."

## Options considered

1. **UAE-first for streaming-economy products** — original hope; rejected on banking-infrastructure grounds.
2. **Singapore lead, UAE follow when banking infrastructure catches up** (chosen) — Singapore double-down for AI-Native + Uber-for-engineers + REIT data products; UAE continues for other commercial purposes.
3. **Hong Kong / China lead** — not seriously considered on this call; existing relationship via cap table (Guang).

## Why this decision

- **Singapore PM has formally endorsed AI in REITs** — white paper to be shared; "PM has spoken out saying that AI is not a chatgpt but more delivering the next iteration of really now forcing REITs to use it... integrated."
- **Singapore is the Asia-Pac hub for financial products** — particularly REITs and real estate funds; "a lot of funds are set up there that don't even have investment in Singapore. They're in the region."
- **Government and union infrastructure aligned with Janus's three-pillar messaging** — Singapore union framing ("can't protect jobs but will protect the workers") maps cleanly to [[ai-native-janus-positioning|Pillar 3]].
- **High-profile advocate inside the government** — Foreign Minister [[vivian-balakrishnan]] (Bonaventure knows him personally, was Ambassador to UAE) is keynoting the AI Engineering Conference 16–17 May with a personally-built LLM wiki on a Raspberry Pi. Strong positive signal and potential introduction route.
- **UAE banking constraint** — batch-vs-streaming-economy gap on the banking side; few-families ownership of the banking system limits open-banking adoption.
