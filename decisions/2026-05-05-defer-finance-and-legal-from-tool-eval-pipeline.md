# Finance/contracts/legal are not part of the tool-evaluation pipeline

## Decision

The tool evaluation framework will stop at sandbox/approval; cost and contract review will be handled by Finance as a separate downstream process, not as a hard gate inside the evaluation skill.

## Why

Expense should not block knowledge-acquisition decisions — a tool can be valuable enough to justify cost, and adding a financial hard gate would bias rejections. Keeping legal/contracts separate also preserves the technical focus of the evaluation framework.

## Evidence

> Speaker 1: I don't think we should have a hard gate because it's about knowledge. You don't want it to stop because it's expensive.
