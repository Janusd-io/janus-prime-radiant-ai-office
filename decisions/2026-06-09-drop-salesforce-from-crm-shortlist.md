# Drop Salesforce from the CRM shortlist

**Decision (made after the 2026-06-09 discovery call; confirmed by Michael Bruck, 2026-06-12):** Salesforce is out of the Janus CRM evaluation. The four remaining candidates are [[attio|Attio]], [[twenty|Twenty]], [[hubspot|HubSpot]] and [[monday-com|Monday CRM]]; three finalists go to Bonaventure and Ann (Finance) for the commercial decision.

## Why

Per [[2026-06-09-salesforce-janus-digital-global]] (§[51:24]–[53:30], demo-gating exchange):

- **Vendor-controlled process.** Salesforce refused a standard demo without first running its own scoping call ("we don't do demos… we've got solution engineers that run the demos"), making the Jun 9 call the third conversation without seeing the product. Andrew pushed back explicitly and flagged internal "big bloated machine" pushback.
- **Standalone-CRM aversion.** The rep stated Salesforce "walks away from solo CRM projects" — a structural mismatch with Janus's start-small posture (~5 licences at signing, agent-operated, scale with country openings).
- **Pre-existing technical trade-off.** Salesforce scored 2/3 on the [[stack-composition-framework]] (Apex reversibility risk) in the [[marketing-stack-technical-writeup]]; the call confirmed the process-weight and ecosystem-bundling concerns rather than offsetting them.

## What this does not mean

Salesforce remains the capability **benchmark** on paper (the role it already held in the May matrix). The drop is about engagement-model and architecture fit, not product capability.
