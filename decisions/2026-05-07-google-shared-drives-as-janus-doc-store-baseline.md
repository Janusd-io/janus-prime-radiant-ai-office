# Google Shared Drives as the Janus document-store baseline, per department

## Decision

Each Janus department gets its own Google Shared Drive (not personal My Drive), with internal/restricted vs. company-wide subfolders, as the canonical storage layer for non-knowledge documents (policies, licenses, brand guidelines, etc.).

## Why

Shared Drives are owned by the Google entity rather than any individual, so content survives departures; they centralise per-department files; and they integrate cleanly with desktop sync. Personal My Drive storage is explicitly rejected because departure causes orphaning and migration overhead.

## Evidence

> Michael Bruck: every, every, every department should have a Google Drive… their internal file.
