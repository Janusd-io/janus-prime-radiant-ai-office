## Summary

Introductory onboarding meeting for Emma McCall, joining as the Janus training and certification programme lead. Bonaventure and Michael demoed the Prime Radiant / Obsidian wiki live. Meeting covered: the BMS engineer certification programme mandate, internal AI tool training as an identified gap, the build-not-buy posture for training infrastructure, and Singapore as the first certification market.

## Key topics

### Emma McCall — role and background
- Hired as training and certification programme specialist. Not an engineer.
- Background: 10+ years at a large corporate (heavy AI restrictions, limited internal tooling). Prior experience structuring certification and L&D programs.
- Primary mandate: design and build the BMS engineer certification programme targeting on-site engineers who install/configure Janus AI for buildings.
- Secondary mandate: internal Janus staff AI tool training (acknowledged as a gap during the meeting).

### BMS engineer certification programme
- Target audience: BMS (Building Management System) engineers, the on-site/delivery tier. Domain expertise confirmed; the gap is AI tool proficiency and Janus-platform fluency.
- First market: Singapore (Keppel building as guinea pig/PoC).
- "Train the trainer" model: onboard senior engineers first; they train juniors and apprentices. Bonaventure framed the programme as certification (not "training") to avoid displacement optics — *"you're not being trained for something new, you're being certified to connect with us."*
- Assessment / certification component: continuous assessment throughout; a benchmark gap-closing exam/test at the end; gamification elements proposed (LinkedIn badges, level progression).
- Training environment: a real building used as a simulation/sandbox so engineers can practice without affecting live systems.
- Speaker 4 (unattributed): emphasised building foundational knowledge base for newcomers to query, and the importance of a structured training environment tied to the real platform.

### Prime Radiant demo to Emma
- Michael and Bonaventure demoed the Obsidian wiki/knowledge graph live. Emma observed the graph view, the knowledge retrieval, and a live query about the BMS certification programme (which surfaced the CEO meeting decision on certification). Key quote from Bonaventure: *"don't be daunted that you have to read everything — it's already read by the AI, and the AI is there to empower you to build that program."*

### Internal AI tool training — identified gap
- Bonaventure explicitly named this as "a big missing piece in our onboarding." Most staff are not using AI tools effectively.
- Specific example: staff were using Claude to create PowerPoint presentations — expensive, slow, inaccurate. Resolution: HTML deck workflow (Janus HTML Deck skill). Michael: *"it costs a fraction, it's practically nothing."* But staff need instruction on why and how.
- Michael: *"I don't know how to do training materials."* Emma's expertise directly fills this gap.

### Build-not-buy posture for training platforms
- Michael and Bonaventure explicitly discussed not adopting an off-the-shelf LMS. Rationale: SaaS platforms are "a generation or two behind"; AI makes bespoke build feasible; lock-in and UI constraints are costs not worth paying. Emma asked about learning framework tools; Michael said use existing tools only for inspiration, not adoption.
- Note: framed as a direction/philosophy in this meeting, not a locked formal decision.

### AI tools and cost management
- Gemini is primary workforce tool (cost-effective). Claude is secondary (10× more expensive; reserved for specialised tasks, especially code).
- PowerPoint via Claude: explicitly called out as a costly anti-pattern. HTML decks are the replacement.
- Agentic posture on Monday.com: Janus uses Monday's APIs but not its native AI. Agents control Monday programmatically; human-facing Monday UI is secondary.

### Geographic rollout
- Singapore first (Keppel building PoC; union sensitivity — explicit commitment to not leave workers behind). UK next. 10 countries by end of 2026.
