## Clean Meeting Summary

Cross-functional working session on 22 May 2026 (~55 minutes). Confirmed participants by name: Simon Tarskih (ISO lead) and Jehad Altoutou (AIO). Additional participants via shared department accounts: Project Management Department and AIO Department. Attribution note: "Project Management Department" and "AIO Department" are Fireflies shared-account labels — individual attribution not confirmed for those speakers; attributed to group/department per CLAUDE.md §5.1 discipline.

**ISO 41-process IMS framework:** Simon has defined 41 draft processes covering three standards: ISO 9001:2022 (quality management), ISO 27001 (information security), and ISO 42001 (AI management). The framework is a working draft — scope will evolve as departments describe their real workflows. The 41 processes represent Simon's initial mapping after analysing which clauses apply to Janus; some may be added, split, or found inapplicable.

**Process documentation methodology (PM dept walk-through):** The group walked through a live example — a 3D modeling task from the Project Management department. Key learning: sources must distinguish between web-based apps (DTX account — the platform used to upload/download drawings; no shareable URL per file) and documents stored in Google Drive (scope documents in a named folder). Simon noted several source entries in the PM dept's process table were incomplete or missing descriptors. The correct pattern: if it's a document/folder, give the location + name; if it's an app, name the app and describe the interaction type.

**Format decision:** HTML tables are confirmed as the delivery format for all 41 process documents. PDF is not required; diagrams are "nice to have" but not required for ISO audit readiness. An auditor checks: does documentation exist, do people know about it, can you show evidence? The table format satisfies this. Simon confirmed HTML is preferred over PDF for AI integration purposes.

**Critical alignment — ISO vs. automation:** The meeting reached an important shared understanding: ISO documentation and process automation are two entirely separate, non-dependent tracks. ISO certification requires documented processes — not automated ones. Automation (whether AI-driven or traditional software) is a parallel company initiative. The group had been mixing these concerns; the separation was made explicit and agreed.

**Three standards in scope:** ISO 9001:2022 (quality/document management), ISO 27001 (information security / data protection), ISO 42001 (AI management system). Future consideration: ISO 14001 (environmental management) and ISO 45001 (occupational health & safety) — flagged as a potential client requirement; deferred to a future IMS expansion phase.

**Unified privacy policy:** Simon confirmed his design: one integrated privacy policy covering all three standards plus applicable legal requirements (GDPR for EU-related data processing, PDPA for Singapore operations). Simon's planned approach: (1) define ISO requirements, (2) define legal requirements, (3) combine into a single IMS policy. Rationale: Janus is building an Integrated Management System — a unified policy is more practical and auditable than per-standard documents.

**Diagram template (pending):** Jehad's process diagram template has been awaiting Simon's confirmation for approximately one month. Simon confirmed in this meeting that he wants to review it. Action: Jehad to send the template; Simon to approve or suggest revisions.

**IMS delivery mechanics:** HTML format confirmed. The IMS is partly in Excel already. Simon will share his example process table with participants so they can validate their own tables against it. Follow-up meeting to be scheduled after teams have reviewed the example.
