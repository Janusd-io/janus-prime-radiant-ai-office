# Everything is Recorded Now

**Source:** a16z News (Opinion), David Haber, 10 June 2026
**URL:** https://www.a16z.news/p/everything-is-recorded-now

## Abstract

The "living context layer" is being built inside companies right now whether they're paying attention or not. Meeting recordings have become the default — not through debate but through bottom-up and top-down pressure. A new category of enterprise software organised around voice (rather than structured text data) is emerging. The model ingesting two years of internal conversation is simply a better assistant than one that read only documentation.

## Key claims

- Most work discussions are now recorded by default; this trend is irreversible.
- The right mental model: onboard AI like an employee — invite it to meetings, let it learn through osmosis. Documentation onboarding is insufficient.
- **Bridgewater** as ahead-of-its-time: recording everything as institutional policy looked eccentric; it was prescient.
- **OpenAI** runs with essentially everything recorded; agents stand in for senior leaders in meetings they can't attend.
- **Granola** cited as the clearest example: better context on a16z culture and investments than almost any other tool because it's been in the room.
- Two compounding advantages of recording: (1) bottom-up force multiplication for ICs; (2) top-down oversight — execs' AI flagging what matters in meetings they can't attend.
- **Verbal culture companies** (Shopify, OpenAI) have a compounding advantage in an AI-native world; the historical bottleneck (conversation context evaporated) is now solved. Written culture companies (Stripe, Anthropic) already captured context by construction.
- Default will flip: "assume you're being recorded unless a meeting is explicitly designated otherwise." AC Priv designation likely for HR/legal exceptions.
- The living context layer is being built now — the question is whether you build the right governance while you still have the advantage of choosing.
