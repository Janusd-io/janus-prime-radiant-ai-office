# Policy on the AI Exponential — Dario Amodei

*Author: Dario Amodei (Anthropic CEO). Published ~Jun 2026. Clipped 2026-06-11.*

Amodei's most substantive policy essay to date. Announces a shift from transparency-advocacy (Anthropic's prior stance 2023–2025) to binding-regulation advocacy. Paired with two legislative proposals from Anthropic: (1) frontier model testing bill and (2) job displacement policy framework.

## Framing: Hobbit and Treebeard

AI advances at exponential pace; policy is Treebeard (takes a day to say hello). The mismatch is painful — in the time Congress acts, AI goes from toy to strategic asset. Policy must activate now.

## Five policy areas

### 1. Regulation and public safety (FAA model)

Prior stance was *transparency* (disclosure, reporting, incident notification). That was appropriate when AI risks were speculative. Now Mythos Preview proved frontier AI poses real cybersecurity and bioweapon risks — risks are no longer theoretical.

Proposed shift: mandatory third-party pre-release testing for models above a compute threshold, across four risk domains: (1) cybersecurity, (2) biological weapons, (3) loss of AI control, (4) automated R&D accelerating those risks. Government gets power to block or defer deployment if testing fails. Structure: FAA-equivalent or "regulatory markets" (authorized private evaluators).

Also: AI companies must protect model weights, red-team, and report critical safety incidents.

### 2. Macroeconomics and job displacement

AI may cause *enduring* job displacement — different in kind from prior tech revolutions because it substitutes for human *cognition broadly*, not narrow skills. Two key points:
- Janus relevance: Amodei says the goal is not to cause displacement but to be realistic about the risk.
- Policy proposals: measurement and tracking (Anthropic runs an Economic Index), pro-employment incentives (wage insurance, retention tax credits, workforce training), long-term support (UBI financed through capital gains tax and company taxes).

### 3. Scientific acceleration (FDA reform)

AI will flood the drug pipeline; the FDA's 7–8 year approval timeline will create a bottleneck rather than a problem of lack of innovation. Agencies must preemptively set standards for AI-based PD/PK modeling, toxicology prediction, synthetic control arms, etc.

### 4. State and civil liberties

Risk: AI could enable either governments *or* companies to seize disproportionate power ("the East India Company risk"). Specific proposals: accountability rules for autonomous weapons; ban autonomous weapons in domestic law enforcement; close bulk data collection / data broker loophole; citizens' right to AI advice during adverse government action.

### 5. Geopolitics (democratic AI coalition)

AI is not a trade policy instrument — it is a strategic asset comparable to nuclear weapons. Nations behind by 3 years in AI face a WWII Marines vs medieval swords disadvantage. Proposed: a democratic coalition sharing chips/SME within the coalition, denying to adversaries, coordinating AI regulation, coordinating biomedical reform, mutual defense with AI, rejecting AI-powered repression as coalition membership requirement. Export controls on chips/SME to expand and coordinate with allies.

## Key claim: "The window is opening"

*"People are worried about AI because they correctly perceive that its risks are real."* Amodei rejects "better PR" framing. Policymakers are unusually open to action right now; the goal is converting concern into constructive policy before the window closes.
