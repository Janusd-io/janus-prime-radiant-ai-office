# Bonaventure Meeting — Reframe Analysis (20 Apr 2026)

**Meeting:** Bonaventure Wong ↔ Michael Bruck ↔ Jehad Altoutou
**Date:** 20 April 2026, 15:11
**Context:** Michael walked Bonaventure through v2 of the CEO Executive Management System design document.

## Headline reframe

Bonaventure did not reject the management system — but he **significantly reordered the project.** The weekly CEO meeting and executive dashboard is no longer the primary deliverable. The primary deliverable is the **ISO-driven workflow and automation foundation** across all twelve functions. The management system is a natural byproduct of getting ISO + workflows + automation right.

His exact framing: *"Don't worry about me at this time. Worry about applying this for everyone to see what you've done."*

## Key reactions per v2 section

- **Core Idea / What You Get as CEO:** Lukewarm on CEO-centric framing. Wants **organisational showcase**, not personal dashboard. "I know where everyone is" by speaking directly.
- **Self-Service Operating Model:** Confirmed strongly. *"No, no fucking Chief of Staff here. We're too lean."*
- **Why Notion:** Partially reopened. Asana is out. **Notion vs Monday.com** is now the debate (not Notion vs Asana).

## New threads he introduced

### 1. ISO as organisational spine (biggest new thread)
- Simon defines which ISO standards Janus will comply with (~1 week)
- Each department documents workflows against those standards
- AI identifies gaps and automation opportunities within workflows
- AI agents orchestrate across multiple workflows and detect discrepancies
- **End state: real-time continuous ISO compliance, not annual audit**

Direct quote: *"Why does ISO audit have to be once a year? Why can't be in real time? ... Just imagine Janus and ISO together. That's power."*

### 2. "Janus standard" Trojan horse
Real-time compliance engine becomes, over time, a standard Janus sells back to the industry. *"We don't change you, we comply to you... but what we're really doing is building our own standard in the future that no one wants will compete with us."* **This is a strategic commercial thread**, not just internal ops.

### 3. The playbook model
Global HQ creates playbooks (combinations of workflows + automations); local markets execute. Connected to staying lean while scaling to ten countries.

### 4. Third-party-first tooling philosophy
*"The reason why I think we should be using third party product is because there's VC money spending the money to build it. Why are we spending the money to build it? So we're just the last mile."* Constrains build vs buy going forward.

### 5. AI Registry as commercial asset
*"When we start talking to our clients and they suggest, I want to use this, we've got data at the back, why you shouldn't be using this. This is also data for us."* **Worth flagging to AI Registry skill roadmap as new use case.**

### 6. Interview-as-hiring-signal pattern
Using transcripts to evaluate a candidate (scored 7.0 → 6.5 after reviewing transcript). Standalone product concept — **not in scope for this project but worth logging.**

## Decisions made / stances confirmed

- **Asana is out.** Move away from it.
- **No Chief of Staff.** Ever.
- **Task-based, bottom-up.** Not OKR/objectives.
- **ISO foundation comes first.** Management system comes second.
- **Simon is the critical path** dependency for ~1 week.
- **Third-party tools by default**; Janus builds the glue/last-mile only.
- **Fireflies-as-input** is the right pattern across the board.

## What this means for v3

v3 should NOT be "v2 with edits." It should be restructured around the spine Bonaventure drew:

1. **New opening:** The goal is an agent-assisted organisational coordination system, anchored in ISO-compliant workflows per department, producing real-time compliance and executive visibility as outputs — not an executive dashboard first.

2. **Phase model changes substantially.** Phase 1 is no longer "stand up the CEO Monday meeting." Phase 1 is "work with Simon to define ISO standards, then per-department workflow documentation with agent assist, then automation identification, then rollup visibility."

3. **Tooling decision becomes precondition.** v3 needs clear Notion / Monday.com / two-tool recommendation.

4. **Add real-time ISO / Janus standard commercial thread** — strategically important.

5. **Drop or compress OKR/objective schema material.**

6. **Add "what we need from Simon" section** — Simon is critical path.

7. **Reframe Layer 2.** Not "rollout down from CEO" anymore. Every department is Layer 1 simultaneously, working off ISO-documented workflows.

## Preconditions before writing v3

1. Simon meeting completed (ISO requirements, workflow doc tool, pilot dept).
2. Tooling decision closed (Notion / Monday.com / two-tool).
3. Bonaventure's answers on at least questions #2, #4, #5 (meeting frequency, between-meeting visibility, error tolerance).
