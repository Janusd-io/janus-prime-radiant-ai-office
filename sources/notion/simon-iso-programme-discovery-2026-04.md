# Simon Meeting — ISO Programme Discovery

**Meeting:** Simon (ISO lead) ↔ AI Office
**Date:** 22 April 2026
**Attendees:** Simon Tarskih, Michael Bruck, Jehad Altoutou
**Duration:** ~1h54m
**Recorded:** Fireflies

## Working-relationship model

Simon owns the ISO domain — defines standards, documents compliance, leads audit. AI Office leads automation and tooling, serves as Simon's technical accelerator. Michael positioned AI Office as Simon's first ISO pilot and Simon accepted. Framing: Janus is currently "a jazz band" (improv) and needs to become "a symphony orchestra" (standardised, conducted). Standardisation unlocks automation.

## ISO scope — Integrated Management System

Three standards integrated into one system:
- **ISO 9001** — quality management
- **ISO 27001** — information security management
- **ISO 42001** — AI management system (new standard; few auditors experienced yet)

One manual, one audit, one set of common clauses. Michael has purchased copies of all three.

## Artefacts Simon will produce (in order)

1. Assessment of current state
2. Organisational structure document (position titles + RACI per ISO requirement)
3. Standards map (process diagram of the IMS)
4. List of ISO standards relevant to Janus (certifiable + guide standards)
5. List of compliance documents (laws, regulations — GDPR central; jurisdiction-specific for Dubai and Singapore)
6. Requirements-to-role matrix (accountable/responsible/consulted/informed per requirement)
7. Integrated management system manual — the single master document an auditor reads

## Audit plan

German certification body preferred (reputable, cross-border accreditations, good for Janus brand). First audit scoped to Dubai HQ + Singapore. UK deferred. Preparation audit before certification audit to avoid critical non-conformity.

Pace: Simon asked for 2–3 days to prepare a full plan. Confident on audit mechanics ("I did it 20 years"); focus is system design and implementation, not certification ritual.

## Tooling and documentation direction

- **Notion confirmed** as central ISO repository — the "digital binder" for the IMS manual and supporting documents. Markdown preferred over PDFs.
- **Mermaid and Figma** flagged as diagramming options for process maps.
- **PDFs discouraged.** Michael proposed banning PDF creation within Janus; Simon agreed manual-entry-from-PDF handovers are an anti-pattern.
- **Specialist ISO tools (Greenlight Guru, Qualio, MasterControl) not needed** at this stage — Notion + Markdown is sufficient unless something breaks.

## Automation philosophy — Simon's colour-coded map

- **Green — automatable:** documentation, monitoring, record control, document control, analysis
- **Yellow — automatable with human oversight:** policy, management review, corrective action
- **Red — cannot be automated:** context setting, stakeholder definition, leadership

Aligns cleanly with AI Office's risk/task-complexity model.

## Key insights

- Simon's background: ~20 years of ISO auditing, former head of a private certification body.
- Michael has standards background (Intel, BSI connections); not a corporate box-ticker.
- Integrated management system saves effort — one manual, one audit, one set of common clauses. Don't let downstream documentation fragment this.
- Don't rush certification, don't delay the system. The audit is easy for Simon; the hard work is designing and implementing the IMS.
- Real-time continuous compliance (rather than annual audit) is in scope and viable per Simon.
