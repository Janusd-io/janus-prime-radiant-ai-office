# "Wiki" vs "brain" — the supporting metaphor needs to match the audience

## What happened

In the 2026-05-08 brainstorm with Andrew Soane (CMO), Michael was demoing the AIO Prime Radiant. Andrew, after seeing the synthesis layer (briefs, derived insights, knowledge graph, pulse), reached for a marker and started sketching a Marketing-domain instance. He labelled the diagram "marketing wiki" — and immediately corrected himself, calling it a brain rather than a wiki. Michael agreed: "It's a brain, it's not a wiki. That's what I'm calling the Prime Radiant."

## The lesson

**The system's proper name is fixed (Janus Prime Radiant). The supporting metaphor that helps any given audience grasp it is not.** For technical audiences (engineers, AI Office), "wiki" accurately describes the surface form (markdown files, frontmatter, wikilinks, knowledge graph). For non-technical leadership audiences, "wiki" pattern-matches to *static* (Wikipedia, Confluence, internal documentation graveyards) and undersells the synthesis / inference capability. "Brain" works because it conveys active thought, pattern recognition, memory + synthesis — something that grows and connects.

## How to apply

- **System name stays:** Janus Prime Radiant (proper noun; Asimov-grounded).
- **Pitch metaphor varies:** "company brain" / "department brain" / "your brain" for non-technical audiences; "LLM wiki" / "knowledge graph" / "institutional KB" for technical contexts.
- **In wiki content** (briefs, project hubs, lessons): use "Janus Prime Radiant" as the canonical term, but allow "brain" as the supporting metaphor in pitches, demos, and external comms.
- The concept page [[llm-wiki]] keeps its name — it documents Karpathy's *methodology* and is correctly labelled for the engineering audience that originated it. Janus Prime Radiant is one *implementation* of that methodology.

## Why it matters

Naming friction in early demos can kill department adoption before the *thing being demoed* gets a fair hearing. For the Marketing pilot, HR pilot, and every subsequent rollout: lead with "department brain," circle back to "wiki" only with audiences who'd find it more legible.
