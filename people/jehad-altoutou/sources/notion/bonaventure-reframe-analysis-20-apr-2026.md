## Headline reframe
Bonaventure did not reject the management system — but he **significantly reordered the project**. The weekly CEO meeting and executive dashboard is no longer the primary deliverable. The primary deliverable is the **ISO-driven workflow and automation foundation** across all twelve functions. The management system is, in his framing, a natural byproduct of getting ISO + workflows + automation right across the organisation.
His exact framing: *"Don't worry about me at this time. Worry about applying this for everyone to see what you've done. That's where I'm trying to get."*
This is a meaningful pivot. v3 of the design document needs to reflect it — not as an additive edit to v2, but as a restructure around the new spine.

## New threads he introduced
These now matter more than most of the v2 doc.
### 1. ISO as the organisational spine (biggest new thread)
In his model:
- Simon defines which ISO standards Janus will comply with (due within \~1 week)
- Each department documents its own workflows against those standards
- AI identifies gaps and automation opportunities within each workflow
- AI agents orchestrate across multiple workflows and detect discrepancies
- End state: **real-time continuous ISO compliance**, not annual audit
Direct quote: *"Why does ISO audit has to be once a year? Why can't be in real time? ... Just imagine Janus and ISO together. That's power."*
### 2. The "Janus standard" Trojan horse
The real-time compliance engine becomes, over time, a standard Janus sells back to the industry. *"We don't change you, we comply to you... but what we're really doing is building our own standard in the future that no one wants will compete with us."* **This is a strategic commercial thread, not just an internal ops thread.**
### 3. The playbook model
Global HQ creates playbooks (combinations of workflows + automations); local markets execute. He connected this explicitly to why Janus can stay lean while scaling to ten countries.
### 4. Third-party-first tooling philosophy
*"The reason why I think we should be using third party product is because there's VC money spending the money to build it. Why are we spending the money to build it? So we're just the last mile."* Constrains build vs buy decisions going forward.
### 5. AI Registry as commercial asset
When the AI tool evaluation knowledge base came up, he immediately saw the commercial angle: *"When we start talking to our clients and they suggest, I want to use this, we've got data at the back, why you shouldn't be using this. This is also data for us."* **Worth flagging to the AI Registry skill roadmap as a new use case.**
### 6. Interview-as-hiring-signal pattern
His anecdote about using transcripts to evaluate a candidate (scored 7.0 → 6.5 after he reviewed the transcript and found the candidate didn't answer the actual question) is a standalone product concept — using transcripts as hiring signal infrastructure. **Probably not in scope for this project but worth logging for AI Office.**
## Decisions made / stances confirmed
- Asana is **out**. Move away from it.
- **No Chief of Staff. Ever.**
- **Task-based, bottom-up.** Not OKR/objectives.
- **ISO foundation comes first.** Management system comes second.
- **Simon is the critical path** dependency for the next \~1 week.
- **Third-party tools by default**; Janus builds the glue / last-mile only.
- **Fireflies-as-input** is the right pattern across the board — he repeated this in several different contexts (hiring, meetings, management).
## Open questions raised
- **Notion vs **[**Monday.com**](http://Monday.com)**.** Active open question. Needs resolution before investing in schema work. (See [tooling analysis](https://www.notion.so/348114fc090c81d79220ef9b4e001c7f).)
- **Where does workflow documentation live?** He asked directly: *"When everyone's done this, where do they put it? This is another part you've got to think about."* He handed the AI Office the repository design question.
- **How is "Red" RAG actually determined?** He asked how we'd know something is red. Accepted "slipping or blocked" but it's under-specified for a self-service system.
- **Terminology collision on "workflow."** He uses "workflow" in the ISO/business-process sense. It collides with AI-agent "workflow" terminology. The AI Office will rename internally — Simon's usage wins.
## Things he pushed back on
- OKR/objectives hierarchy → rejected in favour of tasks.
- CEO-centric framing → deprioritised in favour of organisation-wide visibility.
- Building our own tools where third-party exists → pushed firmly toward buy-and-integrate.
- Strategic-initiative-down schema → wants bottom-up, department-driven, with rollup to the top.
## What this means for v3
v3 should not be "v2 with edits." It should be restructured around the spine Bonaventure drew:
1. **New opening**: The goal is an agent-assisted organisational coordination system, anchored in ISO-compliant workflows per department, producing real-time compliance and executive visibility as outputs — not an executive dashboard first.
2. **Phase model changes substantially**. Phase 1 is no longer "stand up the CEO Monday meeting." Phase 1 is "work with Simon to define ISO standards, then per-department workflow documentation with agent assist, then automation identification, then rollup visibility." The CEO weekly meeting becomes Phase 2 or Phase 3 — emerging naturally once the spine exists.
3. **Tooling decision becomes a precondition.** v3 needs a clear Notion / [Monday.com](http://Monday.com) / two-tool recommendation.
4. **Add the real-time ISO / Janus standard commercial thread.** Strategically important to Bonaventure; belongs in the doc, not a footnote.
5. **Drop or compress OKR/objective schema material.** Replace with task-based bottom-up rollup design.
6. **Add a "what we need from Simon" section.** Simon is critical path; v3 should be explicit about deliverables and timing.
7. **Reframe Layer 2.** Not "rollout down from CEO" anymore. Every department is Layer 1 simultaneously, working off ISO-documented workflows. The CEO view is the aggregation layer, not the seed layer.
## Preconditions before writing v3
1. Simon meeting completed → ISO requirements, workflow documentation tool preference, pilot department selection known.
2. Tooling decision closed → Notion / [Monday.com](http://Monday.com) / two-tool.
3. Bonaventure's answers on at least #2, #4, and #5 from the v2 question set (meeting frequency structure, between-meeting visibility, error tolerance) — these can be asked async, don't need a dedicated meeting.
