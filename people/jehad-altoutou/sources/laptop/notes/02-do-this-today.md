> The single email to send the ISO lead. Three sentences. Unblocks everything else.

---

## The email

**Subject:** PULS — quick reply on First Voice + tooling

> Hi Simon,
>
> Got the deck — will send my [[puls-first-voice|PULS First Voice]] answers by end of week. Also happy to lead the AI/IT tooling discussion (Step 4 on slide 17) — can we schedule 30 mins this week?
>
> One clarifying question first: am I the named Process Owner for **C1 (AI System Design & Development)**, **C2 (Software Development & Release)** and **S2 (IT Infrastructure & Data Governance)**, or is that being split with Michael?
>
> Best,
> Jehad

---

## Why this email and not the long one

You haven't been formally asked anything yet — the deck is a kickoff. Sending a long, fully-drafted response now risks:

1. **Getting ahead of Michael** — Process Owner assignments are leadership decisions, not yours to claim
2. **Locking in tooling before the meeting** — better to have the meeting and let him feel like he co-decided
3. **Looking like you're trying too hard** — the short reply is more senior

The long version (full ISO Figure-1 diagram + tooling proposal + pre-filled First Voice) is ready in [04-FORMAL-RESPONSE.md](./04-FORMAL-RESPONSE.md). Send that **after** the meeting is scheduled and Michael has confirmed Process Owner assignments.

---

## What this email accomplishes

- ✅ Confirms you read the deck
- ✅ Volunteers you for the part you should own (Step 4 — tooling)
- ✅ Asks the one question that unblocks the next four tasks (Process Owner assignments)
- ✅ Doesn't claim authority you haven't been given
- ✅ Doesn't dump 4 pages on someone who's expecting "got it, will follow up"
