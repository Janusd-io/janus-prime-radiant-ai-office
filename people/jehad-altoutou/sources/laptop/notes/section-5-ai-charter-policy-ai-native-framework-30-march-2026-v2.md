# **Section 5: AI Charter & Policy (AI Native Framework)**

**Compliance Standard:** ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (Artificial Intelligence Management System)

## **5.1 The "AI Native" Mandate**

Janus Digital operates as an **AI Native** organization. Unlike traditional companies that layer technology over existing processes, we integrate Artificial Intelligence (AI) as the foundational baseline for all operations. At Janus, AI is not a supplementary tool; it is the primary engine of our operational infrastructure.

Proficiency in AI utilization is a core competency required for all roles. We are committed to the responsible, safe, and ethical development and deployment of AI systems in strict compliance with our Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) and **ISO/IEC 42001:2023**.

## **5.2 Strategic Pillars of AI Value**

All AI initiatives and usage must align with our three strategic pillars, designed to drive value and resilience:

1. **Operational Optimization:**  
   * **Objective:** We automate core administrative and operational functions to eliminate friction and redundancy.  
   * **Requirement:** Employees are expected to identify workflows where AI can reduce manual latency. However, automation must never compromise accuracy; critical outputs require human verification.  
2. **Strategic Innovation:**  
   * **Objective:** We leverage AI to create new capabilities, services, and value propositions that were previously impossible.  
   * **Requirement:** Innovation must be balanced with responsibility. New use cases must undergo impact assessments to prevent unintended harm or bias.  
3. **Institutional Intelligence:**  
   * **Objective:** We systematically capture, structure, and refine data to build a centralized, queryable knowledge base that fuels our AI ecosystem.  
   * **Requirement:** Data integrity is paramount. You must ensure that information fed into our systems is accurate, traceable, and properly classified.

## **5.3 AI Procurement & Evaluation Framework**

To maintain security and interoperability, Janus Digital strictly prohibits "Shadow AI" (the use of unverified or unauthorized AI tools). All proposed AI tools must be evaluated against the following framework before adoption:

* **Strategic Alignment:** Does this tool directly advance a core business objective? Does it solve a specific, high-value problem?  
* **Operational Feasibility & Scalability:** Is the tool technically viable within our existing architecture? Can it scale globally without incurring prohibitive technical debt?  
* **Risk & Compliance (ISO Alignment):**  
  * *Data Sovereignty:* Does the tool allow full export of source data? We must avoid vendor lock-in.  
  * *Impact Assessment (ISO/IEC 42005):* Does the tool process Personally Identifiable Information (PII)? Is there a risk of algorithmic bias? Does the vendor use our data to train their public models?

*Only tools listed in the **Register of Approved AI Providers** maintained by the AI Office are permitted for use.*

## **5.4 Data Governance & Sovereignty**

Protecting our intellectual property and client data is non-negotiable. You must adhere to the following data classification protocols:

* **Public Models (e.g., Free-tier LLMs):** You must **NEVER** use public, non-enterprise AI models for *any* work-related tasks or input *any form* of company data (including internal communications, client details, or intellectual property). Janus Digital provides access to state-of-the-art, secure Enterprise models for all staff. If you require a specific tool or model not currently on the approved list, you must submit a formal request to the AI Office for evaluation; do not circumvent this process by using public alternatives.  
* **Enterprise Environments:** Confidential data may only be processed within approved Enterprise instances where data privacy is contractually guaranteed and inputs are isolated from public model training.  
* **Recording & Transcription:** We prioritize knowledge capture but respect global privacy regulations (GDPR/CCPA). All meeting participants must be explicitly notified and grant consent *before* any AI recording or transcription tool is activated.

## **5.5 Risk Management & Impact Assessment**

In accordance with **ISO/IEC 42005** (AI System Impact Assessment) and **ISO/IEC 23894** (Risk Management), Janus Digital requires a proactive approach to AI risk:

* **Impact Assessments:** Before deploying any AI workflow that significantly affects clients, employees, or decision-making processes, an AI Impact Assessment must be conducted to evaluate potential risks regarding bias, transparency, and societal impact.  
* **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL):** For high-stakes decisions, AI must serve as a decision-support tool, not a decision-maker. A qualified human must always review and approve the final output.  
* **Incident Reporting:** We recognize that "hallucinations" (factually incorrect outputs) are a probabilistic symptom of LLMs. Therefore, users must remain vigilant, strictly proofread, and fact-check all outputs as part of their standard professional responsibilities. Routine hallucinations should be corrected by the user and do not require reporting. However, **extreme cases of model deviation**, recurring systemic failures, or safety guardrail breaches must be reported to the AI Office immediately.

## **5.6 Accountability & Transparency**

* **Ultimate Responsibility:** AI is a tool, and you remain the "Pilot in Command." You are professionally accountable for all work produced under your name, regardless of whether it was generated or assisted by AI. You must verify facts, validate logic, and "pressure-test" AI outputs.  
* **Prohibition of "AI Slop":** The distribution of raw, unverified, or low-quality AI content is strictly prohibited.  
* **Transparency:** We value trust. Internal and external communications that are significantly generated by AI should be disclosed as such, ensuring clarity about the nature of the interaction.

## **5.7 Roles and Responsibilities**

* **The AI Office:** The central authority for AI strategy, training, tool evaluation, and best practices.  
* **Team Leads:** Responsible for ensuring their direct reports adhere to this policy and for identifying new opportunities for Operational Optimization.  
* **AI Management Representative:** The operational lead responsible for maintaining the ISO 42001 management system, overseeing the Risk Register, and ensuring continuous improvement.

*Failure to comply with these governance protocols, particularly regarding Data Sovereignty (5.4) and Accountability (5.6), may result in disciplinary action.*
