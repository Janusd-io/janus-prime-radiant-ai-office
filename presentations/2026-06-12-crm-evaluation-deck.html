Two lenses: business requirements × technical architecture

Each candidate is scored against both documents. A CRM that meets the marketing requirements but fails the architecture test (or vice versa) does not make the shortlist.

Lens 1 — High-level CRM requirements business

Nine functional areas; the non-negotiables (High priority):

  • Account-based, not contact-based — complex institutional hierarchies, multi-stakeholder buying committees (B2B2B)
  • Built for the long cycle — up to 60-month journeys; GDPR/PDPA retention tension managed in-platform
  • Partner-first — co-marketing, deal registration, partner-attributed pipeline
  • AI-native — agentic record enrichment and sync from day one (req. 9.3); "a core architectural requirement, not a future-state ambition"

Lens 2 — Stack Composition Framework technical

Three lenses, scored 0–3; from the marketing-stack technical writeup:

  • Composability — does the data model map cleanly to adjacent tools (standard objects, clean exports, REST-first)?
  • Agent operability — the agent triad: real CLI + robust API + native MCP. Can Claude operate it without dedicated human ops?
  • Reversibility — if replaced in 18 months, how hard is migration? No proprietary languages, no vendor-locked state.

3/3 preferred default · 2/3 viable with documented trade-off · ≤1/3 reject.

Finalist 1 of 3 · recommended

Attio — the agent-native fit

Strongest combined score across both lenses. The relational data model maps directly onto our LP → GP → operator account structure, and the MCP surface was built for exactly the Claude-operated pattern we run.

Where it wins

  • Relational model fits B2B2B hierarchies — confirmed in the Jun 10–11 demos, not just on paper
  • Agentic enrichment (req. 9.3) is the product's native mode, not an add-on
  • Built-in call transcription with per-seat speaker attribution — potential Fireflies replacement (bonus consolidation)
  • Workflows with Xero and Linear API connections demonstrated
  • Slack app, Gmail capture, REST API on all plans

Trade-offs & open items

  • SAML SSO is Enterprise-only — pricing implication if Entra SSO becomes mandatory
  • No explicit AI-training exclusion found in ToS/DPA — vendor confirmation required before signing
  • Reporting shallower than HubSpot/Salesforce — mitigated by our data-parity layer
  • Smaller integration ecosystem than the incumbents

Cost basis: Pro tier $69–86/user/mo; 5 seats ≈ $345–430/mo list.

Finalist 2 of 3 · recommended

Twenty — the open-source challenger

Discovered by the AI Office during the sprint; founder-led demo on Jun 10. The reversibility story is the best in the field, and the price point is an order of magnitude below the incumbents.

Where it wins

  • Open source: schema and data fully inspectable; self-hosting as a permanent exit option (we would still pay for cloud + support)
  • API/MCP-first with role-based access control for scoping agents — clean fit for req. 9.3
  • Fully configurable relational model — companies, people, opportunities + custom objects
  • Credible team: ex-Airbnb founders, prior startup acquired; clients include Bayer (migrating from Salesforce) and PwC
  • Repo code-reviewed by the AI Office: "nicely built, well defined"

Trade-offs & open items

  • Three-year-old vendor, pre-profitability ("build, not sell" phase) — counterparty risk to price in
  • No Fireflies integration yet (vendor ETA: end of June)
  • Microsoft Entra SSO support unclear — to verify
  • Form builder is DIY; nurture/marketing automation immature — leans on our Resend/Claude layer
  • Compliance certifications (SOC 2 / ISO) to verify for the cloud offering

Cost basis: ~$9–19/user/mo; 5 seats ≈ $45–95/mo list.

Finalist 3 of 3

The third slot: HubSpot over Monday CRM

Bonaventure's process requires three competing proposals. Both remaining candidates are credible — but they fail differently, and one failure matters more for a customer system of record.

HubSpot — recommended third finalist

  • The only candidate with native long-cycle nurture, attribution and GDPR tooling in one platform (reqs. 3, 6, 7, 8 all strong)
  • Gate 1 preliminary pass; EU hosting; native Gmail + Slack
  • Honest weaknesses: MCP write gaps vs req. 9.3; costs climb steeply once Marketing Hub Professional enters; ecosystem gravity pulls toward bundling (the 2/3 trade-off)
  • Role in the shortlist: the mature benchmark that forces Attio/Twenty pricing honest — and a real fallback if either fails diligence

Monday CRM — keep Monday, not as CRM

  • Genuinely good at events/RSVP, forms and dashboards; WhatsApp via partner integration is relevant for Singapore
  • But the board-centric data model is the wrong primitive for relationship-centric customer data — the May technical writeup's verdict, reconfirmed at the Jun 5 demo
  • Quoted $65/seat/mo (CRM) undercuts nothing: 3–4× Twenty, comparable to Attio, without the agent-native model
  • Monday stays in the stack as our project-management system of record — we simply don't promote it to customer SoR

Concentration note: making Monday both PM and CRM system of record would also concentrate two critical systems in one vendor.
