The premise

Most of any company's activity isn't producing value — it's coordinating value-producing work.

Reporting, status meetings, approval chains, information routing, context translation. The bigger and more geographically spread the company, the bigger this coordination tax gets — and historically it has been the binding constraint on scalable growth.

Agentic AI is the first technology that automates coordination itself — not just transactions. That's what makes the AIO worth building.

What "AI Native" means at Janus

It's not marketing shorthand for "we bought some licences." The AI Policy (Section 5) codifies three strategic pillars — every AI initiative aligns to at least one.

1 · Operational Optimisation

Automate core administrative and operational functions; eliminate friction and redundancy. Critical outputs still require human verification — efficiency without abdicating accuracy.

2 · Strategic Innovation

Use AI to create capabilities that were previously impossible. New use cases run through formal Impact Assessments to prevent unintended harm or bias.

3 · Institutional Intelligence

Capture, structure, and refine data into a centralised, queryable knowledge base that fuels the AI ecosystem. Data integrity — accurate, traceable, properly classified — is paramount.

These are the three rails. Anything else is out of scope or out of policy.

How AI restructures the enterprise

Peter Drucker's 1954 frame, reinvigorated by Cloudflare's Matthew Prince in May 2026: every role is one of three types. AI doesn't displace all three equally.

Builders

Create products. Engineers, designers, content creators. AI amplifies them — if an engineer can be 10× more productive, hire as many as you can find. Janus hires more builders.

Sellers

Sell products. AEs, BD, customer success. AI doesn't substitute — humans buy from humans they trust. Janus hires more sellers.

Measurers

Everything else — audit, finance, compliance, ops, middle management, reporting. AI displaces them. Janus replaces this role-population with the AIO substrate.

The Drucker frame is the operating taxonomy behind the AIO's mission. JPMorgan, Cloudflare, KPMG are signalling the same shift industry-wide in Q2 2026.

Six non-negotiables every employee should know

The cross-cutting rules with no exceptions. Read once, internalised forever.

1

No Shadow AI

Only tools listed in the AI Registry may be used. Need something new? Submit a request via #ai-internal-hub on Slack.

2

No public LLMs with company data

Ever. Use approved Enterprise instances only. Public ChatGPT / Gemini / Claude (free tier) is not permitted for work tasks.

3

HITL for high-stakes

AI is decision-support, not decision-maker. A qualified human reviews and approves the final output on consequential decisions.

4

Pilot in Command

Every employee is professionally accountable for work produced under their name — regardless of AI assistance. Verify before you distribute.

5

No AI Slop

Raw, unverified, low-quality AI-generated content is prohibited from distribution. Polish or don't ship.

6

Consent for recording

Any AI recording / transcription requires explicit consent from all participants. GDPR, CCPA, and local regulations apply.

How a new AI tool gets approved

Four sequential gates. Fail at any gate and the candidate is rejected or deferred — it doesn't advance to the next stage. The detailed criteria are in the AI Tool Evaluation Framework.

1
Stage 1
Intake & Triage

Anyone proposes a tool via #ai-internal-hub. AIO classifies (Tool vs Infrastructure) and runs a binary Gate 1 — five hard criteria, any failure = reject.

2
Stage 2
Technical Qualification

Mandatory requirements (MCP compatibility, SSO, vendor viability over 24 months) plus a scored assessment. Three outcomes: Pass, Conditional Pass (Head of AIO discretion), Fail.

3
Stage 3
Sandbox & Domain Experts

3–5 day pilot in the Sandbox Environment against a structured test brief. Domain experts run the trial and complete a structured evaluation form. Majority recommend = pass.

4
Stage 4
Approval, IT Handover, Registry

Head of AI Office approval, then IT security review (SSO, network, licence, L1 support). Both must clear before the tool is listed in the AI Registry and announced.

Ongoing review: scheduled re-evaluation every quarter. Vendor changes (acquisition, ToS update), security incidents, and obsolescence trigger immediate review or suspension.

The day-to-day pipeline that holds it all together

The AIO standup is the canonical example of a Layer-2 agentic pipeline. Every weekday, the same flow turns one meeting into structured signal across five systems.

Fireflies — capture the meeting

Every standup is recorded and transcribed. Raw transcripts are the source of truth ("what was said"); summaries are too shallow. Core Infrastructure — every unrecorded meeting is a sensor that was never installed.

Monday — primary execution surface

Tasks created, status changes captured, sub-items tracked, dependencies surfaced. Per the Context Coverage Invariant, every touched item carries a "Description (from meeting notes)" block — no orphans.

Linear AIR & AIP — AI Registry & Projects

New tools dispatched for Gate 1 evaluation. AI Projects reconciled against Monday's execution surface. Linear is the authoritative system of record for AI tools and AI projects.

Notion — the journal surface

Operations Notebook entry appended daily — decisions, next steps, registry/evaluation outcomes. Narrative + history; not the AI-searchable knowledge layer (that's the Prime Radiant).

Janus Prime Radiant — the synthesis layer

Markdown + YAML wiki the AIO maintains as the institutional knowledge base. Each standup feeds a daily inbox entry; the AIO curates it into vendor pages, decision records, briefs, lessons. This wiki you're reading is one.

Janus Prime Radiant — rolling out across the company

Each Janus department gets its own Prime Radiant instance. Same pattern, different domain vocabulary. AIO ships first as the prototype; the rest follow as Layer-2 substrate matures.
