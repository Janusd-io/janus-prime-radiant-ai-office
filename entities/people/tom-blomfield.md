# Tom Blomfield

**Role:** General Partner at Y Combinator; co-founder of Monzo
**Organisation:** Y Combinator (YC)

Tom Blomfield is a YC General Partner (and previously co-founder of Monzo, the UK digital bank) whose five-part self-improving company loop is load-bearing in the [[recursive-self-improving-loop]] concept and [[ai-native-enterprise-restructuring]] brief at Janus.

## Why he's in this wiki

His talk *"The Self-Improving Company"* (source: [[2026-05-21-yc-blomfield-self-improving-company]]) formalised the self-improving-loop architectural primitive at the organisational level. The five-part articulation he gave:

1. Observe (capture signals — meetings, support tickets, sales calls)
2. Learn (structured knowledge extraction)
3. Decide (AI-assisted or AI-led routing)
4. Act (agentic execution)
5. Measure (close the loop back to Observe)

This maps directly to what the AIO is building with Prime Radiant (Observe + Learn layers) + Monday Automations (Decide + Act layers) + standup skill (Measure → back to Observe). His YC-GP-level articulation of the same architectural pattern is the strongest external-validation signal the AIO has that it is 12–18 months ahead of the broader industry (per Michael Bruck's assessment in the 25 May 2026 standup).

## Relationship to Monzo

Blomfield co-founded Monzo as a greenfield digital bank — an AI-native (for its era) financial services company built without legacy systems. The same pattern he used at Monzo (structured data as the coordination substrate, measurement-first culture) informs his YC-level advice to founders building AI-native companies.
