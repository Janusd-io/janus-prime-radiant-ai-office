# Emma McCall

**Role:** Head of Certification/Training at Janus Digital
**Start date:** 29 June 2026
**Background:** Comes from a large corporate environment with highly restricted AI usage ("they try to build their own internal one... but it's really limited"). New to AI; significant learning curve expected alongside her domain expertise in training and certification programme design.

## Role at Janus

Emma's mandate is to build and run the Janus certification programme — initially targeting BMS (Building Management System) engineers who will be certified to connect their domain expertise to the Janus HGTFT/DTX platform. Later scope: internal Janus staff training + external market certification programmes.

## Domain Expertise

Extensive background in corporate learning design and certification frameworks. Key capabilities she brings:

- **Adult learning principles**: known → unknown progression; starting from what engineers already know before introducing new concepts
- **Audience analysis**: understanding the psychological mix — fear vs excitement spectrum; senior tenured vs junior; technical vs non-technical learners
- **Dual-mode learning design**: on-demand knowledge retrieval vs structured skill-acquisition courses — both are needed for different purposes
- **Continuous assessment**: not single final exams but checkpoint-based evaluation throughout learning
- **Gamification and engagement**: badge systems, progress tracking, self-paced iteration

## First Meeting (3 June 2026)

Emma attended her first introduction session with Bonaventure Wong (CEO), Michael Bruck (AIO), and Rosa Wu (PM). She received a briefing on the Janus AI vision and Prime Radiant/Obsidian demo, and saw Rosa's 28-phase PM knowledge base and AWS simulation environment. Her first major task: a knowledge-transfer session with Rosa (Rosa teaches PM content → Emma designs modules).

Emma's opening question was precisely the right one: "What do we want them to be able to understand and do by the end of the learning, and to what quality level? That's where the certification comes in."

## Quotes

> "It's not just about teaching them how to use the tool — it's overcoming the fear of AI, and in some people it will be excitement. But psychologically, there are a lot of things we have to think about when you think about who your audience is going to be." *(3 June 2026)*

> "We always want to start from the point of what they know first of all. This is what us as adults, this is how we like to learn. So we start with things that they're familiar with first of all, and then we start building on that so they gain their confidence." *(3 June 2026)*
