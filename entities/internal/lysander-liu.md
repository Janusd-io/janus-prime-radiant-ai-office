# Lysander Liu

**Head of Project Management** at Janus, on the operational / management level. Reports up through the Euclid Wong + Rosa Wu co-head structure (Euclid heads IT + Project Management + Operations across all three teams; Rosa Wu is Euclid's co-head specifically on Project Management). Lysander runs the day-to-day Project Management practice — workflow, delivery discipline, the relationship with Janus's project engineering and delivery teams.

## Why the wiki has a page for him

- **Authored the canonical [[project-management-digital-delivery-workflow|28-phase Project Management digital delivery workflow]]** — the end-to-end document, captured on 14 May 2026 from his ~60-minute walkthrough with [[michael-bruck|Michael]], [[euclid-wong|Euclid]], and [[spike-zhao|Spike]]. This is the canonical reference content the Project Management Prime Radiant initialises against. Effectively: every line of Janus's documented project delivery practice runs through Lysander.
- **Articulated [[2026-05-14-ai-bounded-role-in-project-management|the 30–60% bounded AI principle]]** for Project Management — "AI handles preparation work as drafts only; the Project Manager stays in the loop on every comprehension and decision." This is now operating doctrine for the Project Management Prime Radiant rollout and a likely template for other knowledge-heavy functions.
- **Surfaced the [[2026-05-14-project-management-document-management-gap|document-management gap]]** during the same meeting — acknowledged that "each Project Manager has their own files" is the current Janus reality and that ISO compliance is therefore at risk. The acknowledgement is what positions the Prime Radiant as the operational answer rather than yet another tool.
- **Project Management Prime Radiant curator candidate** — when the instance stands up (rollout pilot #2 after [[andrew-soane|Andrew]] / Marketing), Lysander is the natural day-to-day owner. The vault is being initialised against *his* workflow; he is the senior practitioner in the room.

## Working pattern

- **Working language:** Mandarin-primary. The 14 May workflow walkthrough was conducted predominantly in Chinese, with English portions for [[michael-bruck|Michael]]'s probes. Future Project Management Prime Radiant content will need to handle Chinese-English bilingual workflows cleanly.
- **Mental model:** workflow-first. Lysander reasons about projects as a sequence of phases with explicit inputs, outputs, and dependencies. The 28-phase workflow he walked through is not abstract — it is how he actually thinks about delivery. The Prime Radiant folder structure being phase-shaped (one folder per workflow stage) is designed to match this model.
- **Where AI fits in his view:** acceleration of preparation work (drafting charters, summarising prior meetings, surfacing decision history). **Not** acceleration of project comprehension — "if you cut corners on understanding, all the risks happen at the end of the project." This is a useful constraint to design Prime Radiant outputs against.
