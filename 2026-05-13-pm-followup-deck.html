03 / 05
The decision we make today

Product vs project — folder structure to react to.

The intro flagged this as the prerequisite to ingestion: how do we separate product-level content from project-level content? Below is a proposal. The aim is that you push back, refine, or replace — by the end of this meeting we have a structure you can start uploading into.

Proposed structure
program-management/ ├── products/ │ ├── <product-name>/ │ │ ├── overview.md # what it is │ │ ├── roadmap.md # where it's going │ │ ├── stakeholders.md # who cares │ │ └── decisions/ # product-level │ └── ... ├── projects/ │ ├── <project-name>/ │ │ ├── brief.md # scope & goals │ │ ├── status.md # live state │ │ ├── meetings/ # transcripts │ │ ├── decisions/ # project-level │ │ ├── raid/ # risks, assumptions... │ │ └── products.md # which products it touches │ └── ... ├── stakeholders/ # cross-cutting people ├── processes/ # PM playbooks └── retros/ # lessons across projects
Why this shape
  • 1 Products are durable. Projects are episodic.
    A product persists across many projects; a project finishes. Putting products at the top level keeps long-lived knowledge where you can find it. Projects link up into products via products.md.
  • 2 Decisions live at both levels.
    A "we'll always do X" lives at the product level. A "we did X this time" lives at the project level. The brain can synthesise across both.
  • 3 Retros are cross-project.
    Lessons learnt apply across projects — not buried inside one. The brain pulls patterns out of them as they accumulate.
  • 4 Stakeholders are cross-cutting.
    One person can be a stakeholder on many projects and products. Pulling them out keeps the relationship-mapping clean.
Things to discuss before we lock
Where does a portfolio sit (above products)? Do programmes go between products and projects, or are they a flat tag? Where does third-party / vendor / partner content live? Where do internal-PM-team artefacts (templates, checklists) sit — under processes/ or somewhere else?
