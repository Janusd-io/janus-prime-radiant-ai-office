The Janus Digital posture that AI is the foundational baseline of operations rather than a layer added over existing processes. Codified in **§5.1 of the [[ai-policy|AI Policy]] v2.1** ([[section-5-ai-charter-policy-v2.1|canonical source filed 2026-05-22]]) and aligned to ISO/IEC 42001:2023 AIMS. (The earlier `section-5-ai-charter-policy-ai-native-framework-30-march-2026-v2` slug used as a placeholder is now superseded by the canonical v2.1 source.)

**Theoretical underpinning** — the mandate's three strategic pillars (operational optimisation, strategic innovation, institutional intelligence) and the non-negotiable rules are the *policy compliance layer*. The *operating economics* behind why the mandate is structured this way is the [[coordination-leverage-model|Coordination Leverage Model]] (Michael, April 2026 draft). The mandate without the model is policy compliance; the model without the mandate is academic theory. Both together give the AIO an operating system. Cross-references: [[coordination-tax]] (the primitive being reduced), [[coordination-three-layer-model]] (where the substrate sits), [[organisational-digital-twin]] (the compounding mechanism), [[builders-sellers-measurers]] (the role-taxonomy companion frame).

## The three strategic pillars

1. **Operational Optimization** — automate core administrative and operational functions; eliminate friction and redundancy; critical outputs still require human verification.
2. **Strategic Innovation** — use AI to create capabilities that were previously impossible; new use cases must clear impact assessments to prevent unintended harm or bias.
3. **Institutional Intelligence** — systematically capture, structure, and refine data to build a centralised, queryable knowledge base that fuels the AI ecosystem. Data integrity (accurate, traceable, properly classified) is paramount.

## Non-negotiable rules

- **Shadow AI prohibited.** No unverified or unauthorized tools. Only tools in the Register of Approved AI Providers are permitted.
- **No public free-tier LLMs with company data.** Confidential data is processed only in approved Enterprise instances where data privacy is contractually guaranteed.
- **HITL for high-stakes decisions.** AI is decision-support, never decision-maker.
- **Pilot in Command.** Every employee remains professionally accountable for output produced under their name regardless of AI assistance. AI slop is prohibited; significantly-AI-generated comms should be disclosed.
- **Recording/transcription requires consent** (GDPR/CCPA).
- **Routine hallucinations are corrected by the user, not reported.** Only extreme deviation, recurring systemic failures, or guardrail breaches are escalated to the AI Office.

The charter sits above the [[ai-tool-evaluation-framework]] (which operationalises the procurement test) and the [[ai-tool-evaluation]] skill (which executes Gate 1-4 in practice).
