**PULS First Voice** is the structured-questionnaire mechanism used to capture each Janus Digital employee's process knowledge as input to the [[iso-ims-puls|PULS]] IMS. Documented in the IMS Development Programme v41 Final deck (April 2026, sources `ims-development-programme-v41-final-pptx` + the PPTX twin).

## Shape

A short form (10–15 minutes) sent to every employee after the IMS onboarding session. Captures six dimensions per respondent:

1. **Inputs & outputs** — what the person needs to do their work (information, tasks, requests, documents, requirements, resources); where those come from (internal predecessors, external parties); what they produce (completed tasks, documents, decisions, services); who receives the outputs (internal teams, external clients/partners/regulators).
2. **Controls and check points** — where in the process activities are checked for conformity with established requirements, and where output quality is assessed before handover.
3. **Tools & systems** — every tool used in daily work (AI tools, software, platforms). Feeds the AI systems register.
4. **KPIs** — what good performance looks like in the respondent's role; shapes how PULS will monitor the process.
5. **What they need to work better** — IT access, information, training, support from other teams, clarity on responsibilities.
6. **Questions & ideas** — open questions, concerns, ideas about PULS.

## What happens with the answers

- Each respondent's process document is drafted using their input — they then review and confirm.
- KPI suggestions feed Performance Monitoring (process M3).
- Tool lists feed the AI systems register and procurement review.
- Needs are addressed in the resource plan or escalated to management.

## Why this matters

First Voice is the **bottom-up component** of the IMS programme — the institutional way Janus Digital ensures process documents reflect how work actually happens, rather than being drafted top-down by management. Without it, the 20-document process architecture risks being aspirational. With it, every process owner has a concrete evidence trail (the responses) from the people who do the work. It also pre-populates the AI systems register that anchors ISO 42001 compliance.
