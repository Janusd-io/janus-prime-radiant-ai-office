Why staged, why now

Starting small is the fastest path to value.

There's a lot this could eventually do — automated news-clipping, CRM, market data sources, campaign automation. We'll build toward all of it. But the wrong move is waiting until everything's plumbed before you start.

Reason 1
You get value immediately
SG launch is this week. The ICP and Persona work has been in your head. Real blockers, addressable now.
Reason 2
We learn what you want
Three weeks of you using a simple version teaches us more about what to build than three weeks of me speculating about your workflow.
Reason 3
The brain accumulates context
By the time the CRM is plumbed in, the brain already knows your ICP, Personas, themes, country plans. CRM data slots into context that's already understood.

~2–3 weeks to a HubSpot recommendation Bonaventure agrees with. ~3 more weeks for SaaS terms + setup. End-May / first-week-June for decision; mid-June live. CRM is not in the critical path. Google Sheets is the interim system of record for Singapore-launch leads.

05 / 12
The messaging spine

Three pillars. Society → Business → Individual.

Every campaign, brief, POV and white paper ladders up to one of these. Same spine Bonaventure articulated on the 12 May CEO call, reordered to lead with audience-of-impact. Drives content categorisation and (when it lands) website navigation.

Pillar 1 · Audience: society
Sustainability & ESG agenda
AI as the lever that finally makes sustainability and ESG actionable at REIT / asset-management scale. Impact on society. PM-of-Singapore framing aligned.
Pillar 2 · Audience: business
AI as a growth engine
Top-line growth and acquisition optionality, not cost reduction. "Keep your workforce; let Janus give you the 10× top line you couldn't have gotten without technology." M&A acquisition thesis sits here.
Pillar 3 · Audience: individual
Augment & upskill the worker
"AI will not replace you — AI with people who uses AI and a company uses AI will acquire your position." Upskilling, certification, training. "Uber for engineers" model. Union framing.
How the spine flows
Each pillar gives the next audience a reason to engage. Society wants impact → business delivers it through AI → workers are upskilled to deliver business outcomes. Order outputs by who you're talking to: investors / asset managers get Pillar 2 first; broad market gets Pillar 1; HR / union audiences get Pillar 3.

Your toolkit

Three tools now. A custom app later.

Cowork is your conversation with the brain. Obsidian is your view of it. Web Clipper is how you feed it articles. Each does one thing well; together they cover Stage-1 use cases.

Primary
Claude Cowork
You talking to the brain. Ask it to ingest things, query things, synthesize things, draft things. Uses your existing Claude account. Note: Cowork has access to your drive; the chat/messaging surface doesn't.
Obsidian
Browsing + authoring surface. See the knowledge graph build up; navigate between related pages; write directly when you want to.
Web Clipper
One click puts an article in the brain's inbox. The brain extracts, tags, and links it. Your most repeatable signal source right now.
Later
Custom dashboard / app
Scoped after you've used Cowork for ~3 weeks and know what views you want pinned. Building before then is guessing.
